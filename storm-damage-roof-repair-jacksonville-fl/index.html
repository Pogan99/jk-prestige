Signs Your Roof Has Storm Damage

Not all storm damage is visible from the ground. These are the indicators that warrant an immediate inspection.

  • Missing or displaced shingles — visible from the ground or in your yard after high winds
  • Granule loss — granules accumulating in gutters or downspout splash blocks after rain
  • Dented or cracked flashing — especially around chimney, skylights, and pipe boots
  • Interior water stains — brown ceiling marks or attic moisture that appeared after a storm
  • Hail impact marks — soft dimples on shingles, gutters, or metal components
  • Lifted or buckling sections — caused by wind uplift that broke the seal strip adhesive

Storm Damage Insurance FAQ

Will my insurance cover storm damage roof repair in Jacksonville?

Most homeowner policies in Florida cover sudden, accidental damage from wind, hail, and falling debris. Coverage depends on your specific policy, deductible, and the cause of damage. Wear-and-tear or maintenance neglect is typically excluded. Our free inspection report documents that damage is storm-related — the documentation that makes or breaks an insurance claim.

How long do I have to file a storm damage claim in Florida?

Florida law gives homeowners three years from the date of loss to file a claim for hurricane or windstorm damage under most policies. However, you should not wait: the longer you wait, the harder it becomes to prove the damage was storm-related vs. pre-existing, and water infiltration compounds quickly. Call us immediately after any storm event.

What if my claim is denied or underpaid?

A denied or underpaid claim is not the end of the process. Our documentation package provides the foundation for a supplement or dispute. Florida has a public adjuster licensing system, and we can refer you to a licensed public adjuster if your claim requires escalation. We do not charge for the initial inspection regardless of how the claim proceeds.
