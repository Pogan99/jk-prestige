Frequently Asked Questions

How often should I have my roof inspected in Jacksonville?

At minimum annually. We recommend twice per year: spring (before hurricane season) and fall (after hurricane season closes). Jacksonville roofs are exposed to extreme UV, high humidity, and regular severe weather that accelerates deterioration compared to northern climates.

What does roof maintenance cost in Jacksonville?

An annual inspection with minor repairs typically runs $200–$500 depending on roof size and what is found. Annual maintenance programs that include inspection, minor repairs, gutter cleaning, and algae treatment run $350–$700 per year for most residential properties.

Does roof maintenance help with insurance?

Some insurers offer discounts for documented regular maintenance. More importantly, documented maintenance records protect you if an insurer attempts to deny a claim citing 'lack of maintenance' as a contributing factor to damage.

Can you maintain tile roofs?

Yes. Tile roof maintenance is especially important: walking improperly on tile can crack it, so professional maintenance prevents the homeowner damage that is common on tile roofs. We assess tile for cracks, mortar repointing needs, and underlayment condition.

When should I repair vs. replace during maintenance?

We present both options whenever both are viable. A roof within 5 years of end of life that needs significant repair may be better served by replacement. A roof with 10+ years remaining life with minor issues should be repaired and maintained. We document this recommendation in writing.
