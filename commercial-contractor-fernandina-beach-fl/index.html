Commercial Construction Services — Fernandina Beach & Nassau County

JK Prestige Constructor Corp has the range to serve all of these market segments with a single point of accountability.

Nassau County Permit Landscape: Two Jurisdictions

One of the most common scheduling errors on Nassau County commercial projects is submitting to the wrong jurisdiction. The City of Fernandina Beach has its own building department with its own permit application system, review staff, and inspection protocols. Nassau County's unincorporated areas — including the Yulee corridor, most of Amelia Island outside city limits, and the SR-200 growth corridor — are permitted through the Nassau County Building Department in Yulee. The jurisdictional boundary is the city limits line, not always visible on standard maps.

Nassau County Building Department: 96135 Nassau Place, Yulee, FL 32097 — (904) 548-4660. Handles all permits in unincorporated Nassau County. Plan review turnaround runs 3-6 weeks for commercial projects; expedite review is available for an additional fee.

City of Fernandina Beach Building Department: 204 Ash Street, Fernandina Beach, FL 32034 — (904) 310-3480. Municipal building department with concurrent review including planning and zoning when applicable. Projects in the Central Business District trigger Historic District Council (HDC) review before building permit issuance.

JK Prestige performs a parcel jurisdiction check as the first step of every Nassau County preconstruction engagement. We pull the parcel folio, confirm municipality or unincorporated county designation, and structure the permit submission accordingly. Discovering a jurisdiction mismatch after submitting costs 3-6 weeks on a typical commercial schedule.

Our Construction Process — Nassau County Projects

Jurisdiction & Regulatory Research

We identify the controlling permit authority (City of Fernandina Beach vs Nassau County), confirm zoning, and flag any overlay reviews — Historic District Council, CCCL, flood zone, or DEP wetlands — before design begins. This prevents downstream surprises that compress schedules.

Preconstruction Budget & Schedule

We produce an open-book preconstruction budget with subcontractor market pricing, a detailed construction schedule with permit review milestones, and a procurement log identifying long-lead items (mechanical equipment, structural steel, specialty finishes). Nassau County permit review windows are built into the baseline schedule, not optimistically compressed.

Permit Submission & Parallel HDC Review

For historic district projects we submit to HDC and the building department on parallel tracks timed so HDC approval arrives before building permit issuance. For Yulee industrial projects we coordinate Nassau County Engineering for driveway and stormwater approval concurrent with building permit review.

Construction & Superintendent Oversight

A dedicated superintendent manages every Nassau County project with daily site presence, weekly subcontractor pull-planning sessions, and a live RFI log and submittal register shared with ownership. OSHA 30-certified field leadership enforces PPE, fall protection, and housekeeping standards throughout construction.

Inspections, Punch-List & CO Delivery

We manage the inspection sequence with both jurisdictions — scheduling, access, and any correction responses. Final punch-list is completed within the certificate of occupancy timeline so tenant move-in or business opening is not delayed waiting for minor close-out items.

Frequently Asked Questions — Fernandina Beach & Nassau County

Which building department handles permits in Fernandina Beach?

Fernandina Beach is a municipality with its own building department at City Hall, 204 Ash Street, (904) 310-3480. Projects in the unincorporated Nassau County area are permitted through the Nassau County Building Department at 96135 Nassau Place, Yulee, FL 32097, (904) 548-4660. We verify jurisdiction on every parcel before submitting.

Does JK Prestige have experience with Nassau County's historic district?

Yes. Downtown Fernandina Beach sits within a National Register Historic District administered by the Historic District Council. Exterior modifications require HDC review before building permits issue. We prepare HDC submittals — drawings, material specifications, Secretary of the Interior's Standards narratives — and coordinate the review schedule to avoid schedule compression downstream.

How far is JK Prestige from Fernandina Beach?

Our Jacksonville headquarters is approximately 30 miles from downtown Fernandina Beach via A1A North or I-95 to SR-200 East. Project management staff, superintendents, and self-perform crews mobilize to Nassau County daily. We have no travel premium for Nassau County work.

What types of commercial projects do you build in Nassau County?

Our Nassau County portfolio covers Amelia Island hospitality renovations, Fernandina Beach historic commercial renovation, Yulee industrial and logistics facilities along I-95, retail and medical office in the SR-200 corridor, and mixed-use development near the Wildlight master-planned community.

Can you handle coastal construction requirements on Amelia Island?

Yes. Amelia Island properties frequently fall within CCCL (Coastal Construction Control Line) jurisdiction and FEMA flood zones AE and VE. We coordinate with FDEP for CCCL permits when required, ensure structural designs meet Florida Building Code coastal provisions, and spec corrosion-resistant hardware and coatings appropriate for the marine environment.
