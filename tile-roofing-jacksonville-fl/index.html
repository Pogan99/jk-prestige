Tile Roofing Services in Jacksonville

Concrete tile, clay tile, tile repair, and full underlayment replacement — the right approach for the most durable residential roof in Florida.

The underlayment is the real story with tile roofs: Tile itself lasts 50+ years. The 15-pound felt underlayment installed under most pre-2005 tile roofs lasts 20–25 years. When your tile roof starts leaking, the tile is usually fine — the underlayment has failed. A full underlayment replacement (tiles removed, new underlayment installed, tiles reset) is often the right repair rather than a full system replacement. We assess this honestly and put the recommendation in writing.

Concrete Tile Installation

Boral, Eagle, and Westlake concrete tile systems. Weighs 9–12 lbs/sqft. Wind ratings up to 150 mph with foam-set attachment. Available in barrel, flat, and low-profile profiles in 50+ colors. Cost $18,000–$28,000 for 1,500 sqft.

Clay Tile Installation

Ludowici, US Tile, and Lifetile clay systems. Lighter than concrete (6–8 lbs/sqft), superior long-term color retention. Better suited for older homes with marginal structural capacity. Cost $22,000–$38,000 for 1,500 sqft.

Tile Roof Underlayment Replacement

Tiles carefully removed, cataloged by section, and stored. Full deck inspection. 30-year synthetic underlayment installed. Tiles reset with proper attachment method. Broken tiles matched and replaced. Often half the cost of a full reroof.

Tile Repair & Mortar Repoint

Individual cracked or broken tiles replaced. Ridge and hip mortar repointed. Flashing repairs. Valley repair. If you have isolated leaks on a tile roof less than 25 years old, targeted repair is usually the right answer before considering full replacement.

Tile Roofing FAQ — Jacksonville

How much does tile roofing cost in Jacksonville FL?

Concrete tile runs $18,000–$28,000 installed on a 1,500 sq ft home. Clay tile runs $22,000–$38,000. High-end European clay or custom profiles can exceed $40,000. Cost is driven by roof pitch, structural reinforcement needs, and deck condition.

What is the difference between concrete and clay tile?

Concrete tile is heavier (9–12 lbs/sqft) and more affordable. Clay tile is lighter (6–8 lbs/sqft), more expensive, and holds color better over decades. For older Jacksonville homes, the structural load difference can be decisive — some need framing reinforcement for concrete but not clay.

How is tile roofing tested for Florida wind?

Florida requires tile products to pass TAS 100 (wind resistance) and TAS 101 (static air pressure) testing. Products that pass receive Florida Product Approval. We only install tile with current Florida approval and install using the manufacturer's specified attachment method.

Can my home structurally support tile?

Many Jacksonville homes built after 1990 were designed for tile loading. Pre-1985 homes often need structural review before tile installation. We assess rafter size, spacing, and current loading during the free inspection and disclose the assessment in writing before any proposal.

How long does a tile roof last in Florida?

Concrete tile lasts 40–50 years; clay tile 50–70 years. The underlayment beneath tile typically fails before the tile itself. Most tile roof replacements before year 40 are actually underlayment replacements with tiles reset, which is why underlayment selection is critical.
