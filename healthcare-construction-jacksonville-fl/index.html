Our Healthcare Construction Process — 5 Steps

Built around Florida's multi-agency regulatory framework and occupied-facility constraints.

Regulatory Pathway Identification

Before design begins, JK Prestige identifies the applicable regulatory pathway: AHCA licensure requirement, FGI Guidelines applicability, NFPA 99 medical gas classification, ICRA class designation, and interim life safety measure (ILSM) requirements. This analysis shapes the project schedule, submission strategy, and construction phasing before the first dollar is spent on design.

Concurrent Permit & AHCA Submission

City building permit and AHCA plan review are submitted simultaneously. AHCA review for ASCs and licensed outpatient facilities runs 8–14 weeks; concurrent submission prevents AHCA from becoming the schedule-controlling activity after construction has mobilized. JK Prestige manages both tracks and coordinates review comment responses to prevent re-review delays.

ICRA Setup, Phasing & Infection Control

ICRA barriers, negative-pressure zones, HEPA filtration units, construction traffic routing, and decontamination protocols are installed and verified before any demolition or invasive construction activity. JK Prestige conducts a pre-construction ICRA walkthrough with the facility's infection control officer and documents compliance at every phase transition.

Specialty Systems: Medical Gas, MEP, & Shielding

Medical gas piping, NFPA 99 inspection hold points, radiation shielding installation (verified against physicist report), HVAC pressure relationship commissioning, and electrical distribution (including UPS, emergency power, and isolated power systems for wet procedure locations) are executed and inspected in the correct sequence before drywall close-in.

Multi-Agency Final Inspections & Licensure

Building department CO inspection, AHCA construction inspection, AHCA licensure site visit, fire marshal inspection, medical gas certification (ASSE 6020), Joint Commission documentation (for hospital projects), and utility release for permanent power and medical gas — all coordinated in the correct sequence so the facility opens for patient care on day one without administrative delays.

Healthcare Construction FAQ — Jacksonville FL

Common questions from healthcare system developers, physician groups, and real estate investors.

What codes govern healthcare construction in Florida?

Healthcare construction in Florida is governed by the Florida Building Code, FGI Guidelines (2022), NFPA 99, NFPA 101, and Florida Statutes Chapters 395 and 408 (AHCA licensing). ASCs, imaging centers, and clinical labs require AHCA licensure. JK Prestige navigates this regulatory framework as standard practice on every healthcare engagement.

What is the difference between a healthcare GC and a standard commercial GC?

Healthcare GCs must understand ICRA protocols, NFPA 99 medical gas requirements, FGI design standards, AHCA licensing processes, and how to construct in or adjacent to patient care areas without interrupting care delivery. A standard commercial GC without healthcare experience will encounter regulatory holds and patient safety incidents that a healthcare-experienced GC prevents in preconstruction.

Can JK Prestige build in an occupied hospital or clinic?

Yes. JK Prestige executes construction in occupied healthcare facilities with ICRA Class IV controls, sound attenuation, after-hours scheduling for high-disruption activities, construction staff decontamination protocols, and daily ICRA inspection documentation. We coordinate with the facility's safety officer and infection control practitioner before every phase transition.

How long does AHCA plan review take in Florida?

AHCA plan review for licensed healthcare facilities typically takes 8–14 weeks for initial review with additional cycles for comment responses. JK Prestige submits to AHCA simultaneously with the city building permit — reducing overall project duration by 6–10 weeks compared to sequential submission.

What does healthcare construction cost per square foot in Jacksonville FL?

Medical office buildings: $150–$250/sq ft. ASCs: $350–$550/sq ft. Emergency departments and hospital inpatient renovations: $500–$800/sq ft. JK Prestige provides detailed preliminary budgets within 48 hours of receiving construction documents, broken out by clinical systems category.
