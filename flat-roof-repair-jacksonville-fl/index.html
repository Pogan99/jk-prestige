Flat Roof Repair Services in Jacksonville

System-specific repairs — not sealant patches. Every repair documented with before-and-after photography.

Why generic sealant fails on flat roofs: Lap caulk and roof sealant products are not compatible with TPO or EPDM membranes for more than 1–2 seasons. They expand and contract at different rates than the membrane, crack, and allow water intrusion at the repair point. Proper TPO seam repair requires a hot-air welder. EPDM repair requires EPDM-compatible splice tape or liquid membrane. We use the right material for each system.

TPO Seam & Membrane Repair

Hot-air welded seam repairs and membrane patches that match the bond strength of original installation. Flashing repair at parapet walls, HVAC curbs, and penetrations. Proper TPO-compatible materials throughout.

EPDM Repair & Re-Flashing

EPDM splice tape and liquid EPDM flashing for membrane repairs. Seam bonding with EPDM-compatible lap splice adhesive. Parapet wall cap flashing replacement. Drain collar repair and replacement.

Modified Bitumen Repair

Torch-applied or cold-applied modified bitumen patches for granulated and smooth cap sheet systems. Flashing repair at walls, curbs, and drains. Alligatoring treatment and surface coating where appropriate.

Drain & Ponding Water Solutions

Blocked drain clearing and drain enlargement. Tapered insulation to correct slope toward drains. Ponding water analysis — standing water accelerates membrane failure faster than any other factor in Florida.

Flat Roof Repair FAQ — Jacksonville

How much does flat roof repair cost in Jacksonville?

Minor repairs (small punctures, seam splits, flashing repairs) typically run $300–$900. Larger repairs covering 50–200 square feet of membrane run $800–$3,000. Full membrane replacement runs $5–$12 per square foot installed depending on system type. We provide a written scope after a free inspection.

What causes flat roofs to leak in Florida?

The most common causes: failed seams (TPO and EPDM seams separate with thermal cycling), drain blockage causing ponding water, flashing failure at parapet walls and penetrations, and membrane punctures from foot traffic or HVAC work. UV degradation accelerates membrane breakdown in Florida's sun exposure.

How do I know if my flat roof needs repair or full replacement?

If more than 25% of the membrane surface shows cracking, alligatoring, or subsurface moisture saturation, replacement is more cost-effective. Single seam failures and isolated punctures almost always warrant repair. We present both options in writing and recommend honestly.

Can you repair TPO roofing in Jacksonville?

Yes. TPO seam repairs require a hot-air welder to properly re-weld failed seams. Lap caulk is a temporary patch. We use the same welding equipment used during original installation to ensure repair bond strength matches the original seam.
