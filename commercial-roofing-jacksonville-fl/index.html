Commercial Roofing Systems We Install

We match the right system to your building, use, and budget — with manufacturer-backed warranties on every installation.

MOST POPULAR

TPO Roofing

Thermoplastic polyolefin single-ply membrane — the preferred system for commercial flat roofs in Florida. Highly reflective white surface reduces cooling loads significantly. Heat-welded seams create a monolithic, watertight barrier. Carlisle SynTec and Firestone TPO systems with 15–25 year NDL warranties available.

EPDM Roofing

Ethylene propylene diene monomer rubber membrane, proven for decades on commercial buildings. Excellent UV and ozone resistance, flexible in temperature extremes, and highly durable. Ideal for irregular rooflines and buildings with extensive penetrations. Fully adhered and ballasted systems available.

Modified Bitumen

Two-ply or three-ply systems using APP or SBS-modified asphalt. Torch-applied or cold-adhesive installation. High puncture resistance — preferred for roofs with regular foot traffic from HVAC maintenance. Can be surfaced with granules, aluminum coating, or cap sheet.

Tapered Insulation & Drain Design

Flat roofs fail from ponding water. We design proper slope using tapered polyiso insulation and correctly sized interior drains, scuppers, and overflow outlets sized to Florida rainfall intensity. Correct drainage design is the foundation of commercial roof longevity.

Commercial Roofing FAQ

What is the best commercial roofing system for Jacksonville's climate?

TPO is the most widely specified commercial membrane in Florida for good reason: its white reflective surface significantly reduces cooling loads in our high-heat climate, heat-welded seams perform well through thermal cycling, and Carlisle SynTec and Firestone systems are engineered for hurricane-zone wind uplift requirements. For buildings with high foot traffic, modified bitumen offers superior puncture resistance. We assess your building and make a specific recommendation after the free inspection.

How do you handle roof drainage on flat commercial roofs?

Proper drainage design is the most important factor in flat roof longevity. We perform a drain count and mapping exercise during the inspection, assess current drain sizes against Florida rainfall intensity data (typically 10-year/1-hour storm events), and specify tapered insulation to direct water to drains at a minimum 1/4-inch-per-foot slope. Overflow drains and scuppers are also evaluated and added where code requires.

Can you work around our business hours to minimize disruption?

Yes. Commercial roofing can be scheduled to minimize impact on your tenants, customers, and employees. We can work early morning, evening, or weekend hours for noise-sensitive phases. For occupied buildings, we work in sections and ensure no area is left exposed overnight. Coordination with property management, tenant notification letters, and phase planning are all included in our pre-construction process.
