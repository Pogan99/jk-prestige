Medical Office Construction Services — Jacksonville FL

Technical expertise for the most regulatory-intensive outpatient construction environment.

ICRA-Compliant Construction

Infection Control Risk Assessment implementation: negative-pressure temporary enclosures, HEPA filtration units, sealed construction penetrations, dedicated construction traffic corridors, worker decontamination protocols, and daily ICRA inspection logs — required for all Class III and IV construction activities in occupied healthcare facilities.

Medical Gas Systems

Coordination of NFPA 99-compliant medical gas piping (O2, medical air, vacuum, WAGD, N2O, CO2) through pre-qualified ASSE 6010/6020-certified contractors. We manage framing and drywall sequencing to allow medical gas inspections at all required hold points before wall close-in.

Imaging Suite Construction

Lead-lined CMU or lead sheet-in-stud wall construction per the radiation physicist's shielding report, RF shielding for MRI suites, structural reinforcement for imaging equipment loads (CT scanners, fluoroscopy), and coordination of equipment vendor installation sequences with the GC's schedule of values milestones.

AHCA Plan Review & Licensure

Florida Agency for Health Care Administration plan review submission for licensed outpatient facilities (ASCs, clinical labs, imaging centers, home health offices), response to AHCA review comments, coordination of AHCA construction inspections, and integration of AHCA licensure timeline into the project CO schedule.

Clinical Plumbing & HVAC

Scrub sink rough-in, sterilizer connections (steam, water, drain), clinical handwash sink placement per FGI Guidelines, infection-control HVAC pressure relationships (positive/negative pressure rooms), HEPA filtration for procedure spaces, and air-change rate verification per the FGI 2022 Guidelines for Design and Construction of Outpatient Facilities.

Our Medical Office Construction Process — 5 Steps

Designed around Florida's multi-agency medical facility approval requirements.

Regulatory Analysis & Preconstruction Budget

JK Prestige begins every medical project by identifying the applicable regulatory pathway: AHCA licensure requirement (if any), FGI Guidelines applicability, NFPA 99 medical gas requirements, and ICRA class designation for the construction activity in the existing building. This analysis drives the project budget, schedule, and submission strategy before the architect finalizes construction documents.

Parallel Permit & AHCA Submission

City building permit and AHCA plan review submissions are initiated simultaneously. AHCA review cycles in Florida can run 8–12 weeks; submitting in parallel with the building permit prevents AHCA from becoming the schedule-controlling activity after construction has already begun — a common and expensive mistake on medical projects.

ICRA Setup & Mobilization

Before any demolition or construction activity in an occupied healthcare building, JK Prestige installs ICRA barriers, establishes negative-pressure zones with HEPA filtration, routes construction personnel through designated corridors, and conducts a pre-construction ICRA walkthrough with the facility's infection control officer and environmental services team.

Medical Gas, MEP, & Specialty Systems

Medical gas rough-in, inspections (level 1 and level 2 per NFPA 99), and final testing are sequenced against the framing and drywall schedule. Lead shielding installation is completed and verified against the physicist's report before drywall. HVAC pressure relationship testing is completed before occupancy inspection to verify infection-control specifications are met.

Multi-Agency Inspection & Licensure

Building department final inspection, AHCA construction inspection, medical gas final testing and certification (ASSE 6020), fire marshal inspection, and AHCA licensure site visit are coordinated in the correct sequence to achieve both the building CO and the AHCA license simultaneously — enabling the facility to open for patient care on day one.

Medical Office Construction FAQ — Jacksonville FL

Common questions from physician groups, healthcare developers, and medical property managers.

What is ICRA compliance and why does it matter?

ICRA (Infection Control Risk Assessment) identifies construction activities that could disperse airborne pathogens into areas occupied by immunocompromised patients. JK Prestige implements ICRA Class III and IV controls for medical construction in occupied healthcare settings: negative-pressure enclosures, HEPA filtration, sealed penetrations, and daily inspection logs.

What agency inspects medical office construction in Florida?

Medical offices and ASCs are inspected and licensed by the Florida Agency for Health Care Administration (AHCA). AHCA requires pre-construction plan review for licensed outpatient facilities. JK Prestige coordinates AHCA plan review submission and inspection scheduling alongside the city building permit process.

What does medical office construction cost per square foot in Jacksonville FL?

Medical office construction in Jacksonville typically ranges from $200 to $400 per square foot, depending on procedure rooms, medical gas systems, imaging suites, and AHCA-required finishes. Standard medical offices without imaging or procedures (primary care, PT, behavioral health) can be built for $150–$250/sq ft.

Can JK Prestige install medical gas systems?

JK Prestige coordinates medical gas piping systems through pre-qualified ASSE 6010/6020-certified contractors. We manage cross-discipline coordination between the medical gas contractor, structural framing, MEP rough-in, and drywall close-in to maintain NFPA 99 inspection sequencing.

How long does a medical office buildout take in Jacksonville?

A standard medical office TI typically runs 14–20 weeks from permit issuance to CO, including AHCA review time. Projects with imaging suites, procedure rooms, or ASC licensure requirements add 4–8 weeks for additional AHCA review cycles and specialized inspections.
