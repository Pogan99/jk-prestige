Our Office Renovation Process — 5 Steps

From preconstruction budget through punch-list, a process built for occupied-building realities.

Existing Conditions Assessment & Budget

We conduct a thorough existing-conditions walk with the architect or owner's representative to document MEP infrastructure, structural constraints, asbestos/lead paint testing requirements, and ADA path-of-travel triggers. This assessment drives a detailed line-item budget issued within 48 hours, so capital allocation decisions are made on real numbers.

Phasing Plan & Permit Procurement

We develop a phasing plan that sequences renovation zones against the building's occupancy and lease obligations, then submit permit drawings to the City of Jacksonville Building Inspection Division. Phasing is documented in the project schedule and communicated to building management with 30-day advance notice for each phase transition.

Demolition & Hazardous Materials Abatement

Selective demolition is executed under OSHA 29 CFR 1926 Subpart T protocols. Where pre-renovation testing identifies asbestos-containing materials (ACM) or lead-based paint, we coordinate licensed Florida-certified abatement contractors with proper regulatory notifications and air monitoring before structural demolition proceeds.

Rough-In, Framing & Systems Installation

Self-perform framing and drywall crews work within the approved phasing plan. MEP rough-in is coordinated against the framing schedule using a pull-plan, with inspection hold points clearly identified in the project schedule so framing close-in never delays MEP inspections. Weekly OAC meetings keep all stakeholders current on progress and upcoming phase transitions.

Finishes, Close-Out & CO

Finish trades — flooring, paint, millwork, hardware, specialty systems — are sequenced to protect installed work and maintain cleanliness in adjacent occupied areas. JK Prestige conducts an internal pre-punch walk before the formal architect and owner punch, targeting zero deficiency carryover to the close-out inspection. CO package and as-built documentation are delivered within five business days of final inspection approval.

Office Renovation FAQ — Jacksonville FL

Common questions from property managers, corporate real estate teams, and developers.

Can you renovate our office while employees are still working?

Yes. JK Prestige regularly executes phased office renovations in occupied buildings. We use temporary dust-containment barriers, negative-pressure HEPA filtration, after-hours scheduling for high-disruption activities, and sequenced phasing so only a portion of the floor is offline at any time.

What does a commercial office renovation typically cost in Jacksonville FL?

A standard commercial office renovation in Jacksonville ranges from $60 to $150 per square foot depending on the scope of MEP work, ceiling and flooring systems, millwork complexity, and whether the building's base infrastructure requires upgrading. JK Prestige provides a detailed line-item estimate within 48 hours of receiving plans.

Do you handle ADA accessibility upgrades during an office renovation?

Yes. When a renovation triggers the 20% accessibility path-of-travel requirement under Florida Building Code Chapter 11, JK Prestige coordinates the required accessible route upgrades — restroom compliance, entrance ramp/door hardware, parking, and signage — as part of the renovation scope.

How long does a 10,000 sq ft office renovation take?

A 10,000 sq ft office renovation in Jacksonville typically runs 10–16 weeks from permit issuance to substantial completion, depending on the extent of MEP system replacement, millwork lead times, and phasing requirements. Cosmetic renovations can close out in 6–8 weeks.

What permits are required for an office renovation in Jacksonville?

Most commercial office renovations require a building permit, mechanical permit, electrical permit, and plumbing permit if restrooms or break rooms are modified. JK Prestige manages all permit applications, plan review responses, and inspection scheduling through the City of Jacksonville's Building Inspection Division.
