Wind Damage FAQ — Jacksonville

What wind speeds cause roof damage in Jacksonville?

Standard 3-tab shingles are rated for 60–70 mph winds. Architectural shingles carry 110–130 mph ratings. However, shingles that are improperly nailed, older than 15 years, or have failed seal strips can lift in winds as low as 40–50 mph. Thunderstorm-associated gusts in Northeast Florida regularly exceed 50 mph.

Does homeowner's insurance cover wind damage in Florida?

Yes, in virtually all cases. Wind damage is a standard covered peril under Florida homeowner's policies. However, insurers may attempt to attribute damage to age-related failure rather than a specific wind event. Professional documentation tying damage to a specific storm protects your claim.

How do I know if my roof has wind damage?

Visible signs from the ground: missing shingles (bare dark patches), lifted shingles, blown ridge cap. Less visible: creased shingles, shingles with broken seal strips, lifted flashing. A professional inspection is the only reliable way to assess full extent of wind damage.

How quickly should I repair wind damage?

Immediately. Missing shingles expose the deck directly to Florida's rain. A single missing shingle can allow enough water intrusion during one heavy rain to cause mold growth in the attic within 48 hours and stain interior ceilings. Emergency tarp coverage followed by permanent repair is the correct sequence.
