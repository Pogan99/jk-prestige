Mixed-Use Construction in Jacksonville: Complexity, Cost, and What to Plan For

Mixed-use construction in Jacksonville requires managing the complexity of two or more building occupancy types under one roof — each with its own code requirements, fire separation assemblies, mechanical systems, and utility metering.

JK Prestige has delivered mixed-use projects in Northeast Florida that include ground-floor retail and restaurant space with residential units above. We manage the full scope from design coordination and permits through construction, certificate of occupancy, and individual unit delivery.

Jacksonville's urban core markets — Riverside, San Marco, Springfield, and the Beaches — have seen growing demand for mixed-use projects that activate street-level retail while delivering residential units within walkable distance of amenities.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does JK Prestige build mixed-use projects in Jacksonville?
Yes — ground-floor retail with residential or office above, throughout Jacksonville and Northeast Florida.
What makes mixed-use construction more complex than single-use commercial?
Multiple occupancy classifications, fire separation between residential and commercial, independent MEP systems, and separate utility metering for each use.
How much does mixed-use construction cost in Jacksonville?
Mixed-use construction typically runs $150–$280/sq ft depending on number of stories, unit count, and parking structure.
How long does a mixed-use project take in Jacksonville?
Most mixed-use projects take 12–24 months from permit approval to certificate of occupancy.
Does JK Prestige do design-build for mixed-use in Jacksonville?
Yes — single-source design-build for mixed-use projects delivers faster schedules and better cost control than separate architect/GC contracting.
