Frequently Asked Questions

What type of foundation is standard in Jacksonville FL?

Most Jacksonville residential construction uses slab-on-grade — a concrete slab poured directly on prepared ground. Stem wall foundations are used in flood zones or on lots with significant grade changes.

How thick should a concrete slab be in Florida?

Residential slabs are typically 4 inches thick with 6-inch thickening at the perimeter and under load-bearing walls. Commercial and garage slabs are often 5 or 6 inches.

How long does a concrete foundation take to cure?

Concrete reaches adequate strength for framing in 7 days and full design strength at 28 days. We schedule inspections and framing starts around the 7-day mark in most cases.

Do you handle soil compaction testing?

Yes. We coordinate with third-party geotechnical firms for soil reports and compaction testing as required by the building department.

Can you pour foundations in Florida's rainy season?

Yes, with proper scheduling. We monitor weather windows and use curing compounds and covers to protect fresh pours from rain.
