Commercial Kitchen Construction in Jacksonville: What It Takes to Get It Right

Commercial kitchen construction requires sequencing that most general contractors get wrong: the grease trap is installed before the slab in many configurations, the gas rough-in has to be in before finishes start, and the hood exhaust dictates ceiling height constraints throughout the kitchen.

JK Prestige builds commercial kitchens in the right sequence — coordinating with health department pre-plan reviews, equipment suppliers, and the Jacksonville Environmental Protection Board for grease trap compliance.

Jacksonville's food service market continues to grow, with new restaurant concepts, food halls, and ghost kitchen facilities opening regularly. A well-built kitchen is the foundation of a profitable operation.
