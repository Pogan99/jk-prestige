Our TI Delivery Process — 5 Steps

A repeatable, transparent process that protects landlord allowances and tenant move-in dates.

Preconstruction & Budget Validation

We review the lease TI allowance, the architect's construction documents (or design-assist bridging documents), and the building's existing MEP infrastructure to identify gaps between allowance and actual scope. We issue a line-item Schedule of Values within 48 hours, flagging allowance risks before the lease is executed.

Permit Procurement & Landlord Coordination

We obtain the building owner's Letter of Authorization, submit permit drawings to the City of Jacksonville Building Inspection Division, and track plan review comments through the online portal. Simultaneously, we coordinate with the property manager on site logistics, freight elevator access windows, and after-hours work approvals.

Mobilization & MEP Rough-In

Self-perform framing and drywall crews mobilize immediately upon permit issuance. MEP rough-in is sequenced against the framing schedule using the project's pull-plan, ensuring mechanical, electrical, plumbing, and low-voltage rough-in inspections clear before drywall close-in — eliminating costly late-stage rework.

Finishes, Millwork & Systems Integration

Ceiling grid, ACT tile, flooring, paint, millwork, doors and hardware, storefront systems, and tenant-specified specialty items are installed and coordinated against the RFI log and approved submittal register. Low-voltage, AV, and security contractors work under our supervision within the integrated schedule.

Punch-List, CO & Tenant Turnover

We conduct an internal pre-punch walk, resolve all deficiencies before the formal owner/architect punch, and coordinate all final inspections — mechanical, electrical, plumbing, fire, and building — to achieve Certificate of Occupancy. Close-out documents, as-builts, and warranty packages are delivered to both landlord and tenant within five business days of CO.

Tenant Improvement FAQ — Jacksonville FL

Answers to the questions landlords, tenant reps, and developers ask most.

What does a tenant improvement contractor do?

A TI contractor executes interior buildout work within an existing shell or previously occupied space on behalf of a landlord or incoming tenant. Scope typically includes demising walls, MEP rough-in and finish, ceiling systems, flooring, millwork, and code-required accessibility upgrades. The GC coordinates landlord-work allowances, tenant-work permits, and occupancy sequencing with the building owner.

How long does a typical commercial TI project take in Jacksonville FL?

A standard 5,000–15,000 sq ft office TI in Jacksonville typically runs 8–14 weeks from permit issuance to certificate of occupancy, depending on MEP complexity, millwork lead times, and the tenant's IT/data infrastructure requirements. Medical TI adds 4–8 weeks for ICRA compliance and medical-gas inspections.

Who pulls permits for a tenant improvement — the landlord or the tenant?

Either party can be the permit applicant, but in most Jacksonville commercial leases the tenant's GC pulls the building permit on behalf of the tenant. The GC coordinates with the property manager to obtain a letter of authorization from the building owner, which the City of Jacksonville's Building Inspection Division requires before issuing the permit.

What is a typical tenant improvement allowance (TIA) in Jacksonville?

Jacksonville TI allowances for Class A office space typically range from $40 to $80 per square foot, depending on the building vintage, landlord leverage, and lease term. For medical office, allowances can reach $80–$120/sq ft given higher MEP and code requirements. JK Prestige provides detailed Schedule of Values early in lease negotiations so both landlord and tenant understand cost exposure before signing.

Can JK Prestige self-perform framing and drywall on TI projects?

Yes. JK Prestige self-performs light-gauge steel framing, drywall, and roofing — the three trades that most directly affect TI schedule. Self-performance reduces dependency on subcontractor availability in a tight Jacksonville labor market and gives us direct control over quality and sequencing.

Does JK Prestige handle occupied-building TI projects?

Yes. We routinely execute phased TI work in occupied multi-tenant buildings, including after-hours MEP work, temporary dust barriers and negative-pressure enclosures, and coordination with building operations staff to protect existing HVAC zones, fire alarm zones, and egress paths during construction.
