Our Metal Building Construction Process — 5 Steps

A parallel procurement and permitting strategy that delivers faster than sequential approaches.

Manufacturer Selection & Purchase Order

JK Prestige selects the appropriate metal building manufacturer based on clear height requirements, wind load rating, bay spacing, and budget — and initiates the purchase order at or before permit submission. Manufacturing lead times of 10–14 weeks are the controlling factor on metal building schedules; delaying procurement until after permit issuance adds those weeks directly to the delivery date.

Permit Submission & Shop Drawing Approval

JK Prestige submits permit drawings simultaneously with the metal building purchase order. The manufacturer's Florida-PE-sealed shop drawings are submitted to the building department for approval, and JK Prestige manages the plan review comment response process. Anchor bolt drawings are released by the manufacturer within 2–3 weeks of purchase order — enabling foundation construction to begin before full building fabrication is complete.

Site Work, Foundation & Anchor Bolts

Site clearing, grading, underground utilities, and foundation construction proceed immediately upon anchor bolt drawing release. Anchor bolt templates are installed, surveyed by a licensed surveyor, and verified against the manufacturer's anchor bolt plan before foundation concrete placement. Anchor bolt location and projection tolerances for pre-engineered buildings are tight — typically ±1/8" — and must be verified before concrete hardens.

Building Erection

JK Prestige coordinates the erection crew mobilization against the manufacturer's delivery date, crane capacity requirements, and site access routing. Primary frame erection, secondary framing (purlins, girts, eave struts), roof panel installation, wall panel installation, doors, windows, and trim are completed in a weather-driven sequence that protects the structure and maintains safe working conditions for the erection crew.

MEP, Office Buildout & CO

Following building erection, JK Prestige's self-perform crews mobilize for office framing, drywall, MEP rough-in and finish, LED lighting installation, concrete slab (if delayed until after erection to protect from equipment damage), site paving, and final inspections. Building, mechanical, electrical, and fire final inspections are coordinated on the master schedule for on-time CO delivery.

Metal Building FAQ — Jacksonville FL

Common questions from commercial property owners, developers, and business operators.

What types of commercial projects use metal building systems in Jacksonville FL?

Flex-industrial, self-storage, automotive service facilities, boat storage, municipal maintenance buildings, fire stations, churches, and smaller distribution warehouses. The common factors are clear-span requirements, cost efficiency versus conventional construction, and relatively fast erection timelines.

Does a metal building in Jacksonville FL need to meet Florida Building Code wind requirements?

Yes. All commercial buildings in Jacksonville must comply with FBC and ASCE 7 wind speed requirements. Jacksonville is in a 150 mph basic wind speed zone. Pre-engineered building manufacturers must provide Florida-engineer-sealed drawings demonstrating compliance. JK Prestige manages shop drawing review and Florida PE coordination for all metal building projects.

How long does a metal building project take from start to occupancy in Jacksonville?

A typical pre-engineered metal building in Jacksonville takes 16–24 weeks total from permit submission to CO — approximately 6–8 weeks for permit review, 10–14 weeks for manufacturer fabrication, and 4–8 weeks for foundation, erection, and finishing. JK Prestige's parallel permit/procurement strategy compresses the timeline by overlapping permitting with manufacturing.

What is the difference between a metal building and a pole barn?

A pre-engineered metal building uses engineered rigid steel frames as the primary structural system. A pole barn uses embedded timber or steel posts as the primary vertical structure. PEMBs are code-compliant and permit-ready for commercial and industrial use in Florida; pole barns generally cannot be engineered to Florida's commercial wind load requirements.

Can I add office space inside a metal building in Jacksonville?

Yes. JK Prestige regularly builds office components inside metal building warehouses — either as a mezzanine over the warehouse floor or as a ground-level office block. JK Prestige self-performs the light-gauge steel framing, drywall, and interior finishes for the office component, coordinating with the primary metal building frame and secondary framing layout.
