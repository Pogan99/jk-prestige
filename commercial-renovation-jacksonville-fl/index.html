Our Commercial Renovation Process — 5 Steps

A preconstruction-intensive process that surfaces hidden scope before mobilization.

Existing Conditions Assessment & Hazmat Survey

JK Prestige conducts an existing-conditions walk to document MEP infrastructure, structural constraints, and potential hazardous materials. For pre-1980 buildings, we coordinate asbestos and lead-based paint testing before permit submission — because abatement scope and timeline must be incorporated into the project budget and schedule before the contract is executed.

Code Analysis & Phasing Plan

We analyze whether the renovation scope triggers Florida Building Code's 50% rule (which requires full building code compliance when renovation value exceeds 50% of building replacement value), evaluate ADA path-of-travel upgrade requirements, and develop a phasing plan that sequences renovation zones against the building's occupancy and lease obligations.

Permit Submission & Subcontractor Pre-Qualification

Building, mechanical, electrical, plumbing, fire, and abatement permit submissions are coordinated with the phasing plan. During the permit review period, JK Prestige pre-qualifies MEP and specialty subcontractors, issues RFPs, and awards subcontracts — so subcontractors are under contract and ready to mobilize on the day the permit is issued.

Abatement, Demolition & Rough-In

Abatement (if required) precedes demolition, with air monitoring and regulatory clearance documentation before general demolition begins. Selective demolition is executed under containment protocols that protect adjacent occupied areas. MEP rough-in, framing, and drywall proceed in the sequence established by the pull-plan, with inspection hold points clearly scheduled.

Finishes, Final Inspections & Occupancy

Interior finish trades — flooring, paint, millwork, ceilings, hardware — are sequenced to protect installed work and maintain cleanliness in adjacent occupied areas. JK Prestige conducts an internal pre-punch walk before the formal owner/architect punch, targeting zero deficiency carryover. Final inspections and CO are coordinated against the tenant's move-in date.

Commercial Renovation FAQ — Jacksonville FL

Common questions from property managers, developers, and corporate real estate teams.

What types of commercial renovation does JK Prestige handle?

Office buildings, retail and restaurant spaces, industrial facilities, and institutional buildings across all occupancy types. We self-perform framing and drywall — the critical-path trades on virtually every commercial renovation — and coordinate MEP, fire protection, flooring, and specialty subcontractors under a unified pull-plan schedule.

How does JK Prestige manage renovation in an occupied commercial building?

Temporary dust containment barriers, negative-pressure HEPA filtration, after-hours and weekend scheduling for high-disruption activities, HVAC zone isolation, fire alarm zone bypass coordination, and tenant communication with minimum 48-hour advance notice of each phase transition. We develop the phasing plan with building management before mobilization.

What permits are required for commercial renovation in Jacksonville FL?

Most commercial renovations require building, mechanical, electrical, and plumbing permits. Fire protection and fire alarm permits may also be required. When renovation scope exceeds 50% of building replacement value, Florida Building Code requires full building code compliance — a threshold JK Prestige evaluates during preconstruction.

What is the typical cost of a commercial renovation per square foot in Jacksonville?

Office renovation: $60–$150/sq ft. Retail renovation: $80–$160/sq ft. Industrial renovation: $30–$80/sq ft. Medical office renovation: $150–$350/sq ft. JK Prestige provides detailed line-item estimates within 48 hours of receiving construction documents or design intent.

Does JK Prestige handle asbestos and lead abatement as part of commercial renovation?

JK Prestige coordinates licensed Florida-certified abatement contractors for pre-renovation asbestos surveys (required under EPA NESHAP before demolition of commercial buildings built before 1980) and lead-based paint testing. We incorporate abatement scope and timeline into the project schedule and budget before contract execution.
