Single-source design and construction delivery — faster schedules, budget certainty, unified accountability.

JK Prestige Constructor Corp provides integrated design-build delivery for commercial, medical, and industrial projects across Jacksonville, FL and Florida. Design-build is the preferred delivery method when speed, cost certainty, and minimized owner risk are the primary project drivers. Under a design-build contract with JK Prestige, the owner engages a single entity — us — that is responsible for both the design team's performance and the construction execution. This eliminates the adversarial dynamic that frequently develops between a separate architect of record and GC operating under DBB delivery, and it transfers design liability entirely to the design-build team rather than requiring the owner to manage disputes between consultants and contractors. Our design-build team integrates the architect, structural engineer, MEP engineers, and civil engineer under our coordination, ensuring that constructability is reviewed at every design milestone rather than discovered during construction document review. We use VDC and BIM modeling to identify MEP clashes and structural conflicts before permit submission, reducing RFIs and costly field conditions during construction. For Jacksonville FL developers, property managers, and corporate facility directors who need a single point of accountability from site selection through certificate of occupancy, JK Prestige Constructor Corp delivers a fully managed design-build experience backed by eight years of regional project execution.

Design-Build Delivery Process

A structured, four-phase approach that gives owners budget certainty before construction begins.

Phase 1: Programming & Schematic

Owner requirements analysis, space programming, site feasibility, and schematic design with preliminary budget. Decision-point before significant design fees are incurred.

Phase 2: Design Development & GMP

Design development documents, MEP system selection, and GMP pricing established before construction documents are complete — providing budget certainty earlier in the process.

Phase 3: Permit & Procurement

Construction documents completed for permit submission. Long-lead procurement of structural steel, mechanical equipment, and specialty items initiated during plan review period.

Phase 4: Construction & Close-Out

Ground-up construction with weekly owner reporting, schedule updates, and budget-to-actual reconciliation through certificate of occupancy and complete close-out documentation.
