Building and renovating in Orange Park means working with Clay County Building Services for permit review, which has distinct timelines and requirements from the City of Jacksonville or St. Johns County. Our team is familiar with Clay County's process and submits complete permit packages to minimize review time. We also coordinate with Orange Park's own building department when applicable, depending on the project location within the municipality.

HOA requirements are common in Orange Park's planned communities — particularly in Oakleaf Plantation and similar developments. We identify and coordinate HOA architectural review requirements early in the project planning process so approvals are in hand before construction begins. Missing this step can cause significant delays and we never let that happen on a JK Prestige project.

In most cases, renovation or addition provides better value than moving — particularly when accounting for transaction costs, moving expenses, and the premium on larger homes in Orange Park's competitive market. Clay County Building Services issues permits for structural work, additions, electrical, plumbing, and mechanical modifications.
