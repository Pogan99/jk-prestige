Why Bonded and Insured Matters in Jacksonville

Jacksonville homeowners frequently ask whether a contractor is licensed, bonded, and insured — and with good reason. These three protections work together to shield you from financial exposure when construction work is underway on your most significant asset. Understanding what each covers helps you ask the right questions before signing any contract.

General liability insurance covers property damage and bodily injury to third parties caused by the contractor's operations. If a contractor damages your neighbor's fence, breaks a window, or causes a water leak that damages adjacent materials, their general liability policy covers it. Without this coverage, you as the property owner can be drawn into disputes and claims.

Workers compensation insurance is often overlooked by homeowners but is critically important in Florida. If an uninsured worker is injured on your property while performing construction work, Florida law can hold you personally liable for their medical costs and lost wages. Hiring a contractor who carries workers compensation for their employees eliminates this risk entirely.

A contractor bond provides financial security beyond what insurance covers. Bonds ensure that a contractor can fulfill their contractual obligations. In the event that a licensed contractor fails to complete a project or causes financial harm, the bond provides a source of recovery for the homeowner.

Frequently Asked Questions — Bonded and Insured Contractors in Jacksonville

What does it mean for a contractor to be bonded?

A contractor bond is a form of insurance that protects the homeowner if the contractor fails to fulfill the terms of the contract. It provides a source of financial recovery if the contractor defaults or causes financial harm.

How do I verify a contractor's insurance in Jacksonville?

Ask the contractor for a certificate of insurance. A legitimate contractor will provide this document readily. You can also request to be named as an additional insured on their policy for the duration of your project.

What happens if an uninsured contractor is injured on my property?

If an uninsured worker is injured on your property in Florida, you as the homeowner can be held personally liable for their medical costs and lost wages. Hiring a contractor with workers compensation coverage eliminates this risk.

Does JK Prestige carry both general liability and workers compensation?

Yes. We carry both general liability insurance and workers compensation coverage for every employee. We provide certificates of insurance upon request and recommend that every homeowner review these documents before signing a contract.

Is being bonded and insured required for contractors in Jacksonville?

Florida law requires licensed contractors to carry specified minimum insurance levels. However, the minimums may not be adequate for all projects. Always ask for certificates of insurance and review coverage limits before hiring.
