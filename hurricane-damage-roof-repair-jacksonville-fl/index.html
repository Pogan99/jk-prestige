Hurricane Roof Damage FAQ

How does hurricane damage affect Jacksonville roofs?

Jacksonville's coastal position puts it in the path of Atlantic hurricane tracks. Sustained hurricane winds strip shingles, lift entire roof sections, and allow water to penetrate at every unsealed penetration. Post-storm assessment requires a licensed roofing contractor, not just a visual check from the ground.

Should I wait for a storm to pass before calling?

Call immediately after it is safe to be outside. The sooner a tarp is installed, the less interior damage your home sustains. After major storms, roofing contractors in Jacksonville are booked within hours. Call as soon as conditions are safe.

What is wind mitigation and how does it help?

Wind mitigation refers to construction features that reduce hurricane wind damage: hip roofs, secondary water barriers, roof deck attachment. A wind mitigation inspection report documents these for your insurer and can reduce premiums 20–40%. We provide this report with every post-hurricane replacement.

Can unlicensed contractors work on hurricane-damaged roofs in Florida?

No. Florida law requires a licensed roofing contractor for work exceeding $1,000. After major storms, unlicensed out-of-state contractors enter Florida illegally. Verify any contractor's license at myfloridalicense.com before signing anything.
