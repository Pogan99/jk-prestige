Roof Insurance Claim FAQ — Jacksonville

What documentation does my insurance adjuster need?

High-resolution photographs of all damage points, a written scope identifying specific materials and quantities, contractor pricing, weather data from NOAA confirming the storm event, and a comparison between the adjuster's scope and the contractor's scope. JK Prestige provides all of this with every storm damage inspection.

What is the difference between RCV and ACV coverage?

Replacement Cost Value (RCV) pays the full replacement cost minus your deductible. Actual Cash Value (ACV) depreciates based on your roof's age. On a 15-year-old roof, an ACV payment may be 40–50% less than RCV. Review your policy declarations page before filing.

What is Assignment of Benefits (AOB) and should I sign one?

AOB transfers your insurance claim rights to the contractor. Florida restricted AOB in 2023 (SB 2-A). We do not ask homeowners to sign AOB agreements. You retain full control of your claim. We provide documentation support but you remain the claimant throughout.

How long do I have to file a storm damage claim in Florida?

Florida Statute 627.70132 requires windstorm and related claims to be reported within 3 years of the date of loss. However, waiting weakens your claim. File as soon as damage is identified.

Should I call a public adjuster or a roofing contractor first?

Start with a licensed roofing contractor experienced with storm damage claims. We can identify and document damage, advise whether it merits a claim, and prepare documentation that supports your position. For complex, high-value claims, a public adjuster (paid 10–20% of claim value) may add value — but for most residential roofing claims, contractor documentation is sufficient.
