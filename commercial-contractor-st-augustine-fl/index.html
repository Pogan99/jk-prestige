Our St. Augustine Commercial Process — 5 Steps

Adapted for the City of St. Augustine's multi-layer approval environment.

Historic District & Permit Authority Research

JK Prestige determines whether the project is within the City of St. Augustine, in the historic district (requiring HARB review), or in unincorporated St. Johns County — and develops the permit strategy accordingly. For historic district projects, we initiate HARB pre-application meetings before finalizing construction documents to identify potential material or design review issues before permit submission.

HARB Documentation & Preconstruction Budget

HARB submissions require photographs of existing conditions, proposed material samples, drawings showing the extent of exterior modifications, and a written description of the scope of work. JK Prestige prepares these documents and presents them to HARB, incorporating HARB's required modifications into the construction documents before building permit submission. The preconstruction budget is issued simultaneously, within 48 hours of receiving design documents.

City Building Permit & Utility Coordination

City of St. Augustine building permit submission, plan review comment response, and coordination with St. Johns County Utility Authority (SJCUA) or the City of St. Augustine Utility Department for water, sewer, and utility connections. Historic district projects may have limited utility upgrade options due to underground infrastructure constraints in older street rights-of-way.

Construction with Historic Property Sensitivity

JK Prestige executes St. Augustine historic district construction with sensitivity to adjacent occupied properties, narrow street access constraints, protected historic fabric preservation requirements, and tourism-area noise and dust management protocols. Self-perform framing and drywall maintain schedule without dependency on subcontractor availability in a market where Jacksonville-based trades occasionally deprioritize smaller St. Augustine projects.

Final Inspections & CO

City of St. Augustine Building and Zoning Department final inspections, fire marshal coordination, and utility release for CO. For hospitality projects, DBPR Division of Hotels and Restaurants final inspection and licensure status update. Complete close-out documentation package delivered within 30 days of CO.
