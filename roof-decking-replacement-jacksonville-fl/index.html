Roof Decking in Jacksonville: When It Needs Replacement and What It Costs

Roof decking is the structural substrate that your roofing system is nailed or fastened to. In Florida, the decking's ability to hold fasteners under hurricane-force wind is the most critical element of wind resistance — more than the shingle or tile above it.

JK Prestige discovers damaged decking on approximately 30% of Jacksonville re-roof projects. We charge decking replacement at a clear per-sheet rate agreed upfront — so you know the cost before we start, not as a surprise mid-project.

Upgrading from 3/8" to 5/8" decking during a re-roof is one of the best structural improvements you can make. Combined with an 8d ring-shank fastening pattern, it significantly improves your roof's hurricane performance and qualifies for Florida insurance credits.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I know if my Jacksonville roof decking needs replacement?
Signs include soft spots when walking on the roof, visible rot or mold from the attic, sagging sections, or daylight visible through the deck. JK Prestige assesses decking condition during every roof inspection.
How much does roof decking replacement cost in Jacksonville?
Decking replacement runs $3–$7/sq ft for materials and labor, depending on accessibility and extent. JK Prestige includes discovered damage in the scope during re-roof projects.
Is roof decking replacement covered by homeowners insurance?
Storm-damaged or wind-damaged decking is typically covered. Rot from deferred maintenance is typically not. JK Prestige documents conditions for insurance purposes.
What thickness roof decking is required in Jacksonville FL?
Current Florida Building Code requires 19/32" (5/8") minimum for new construction. Older homes may have thinner decking that doesn't meet current code.
Does JK Prestige charge extra for decking replacement during a re-roof?
Decking replacement is charged as a line item in our contracts at a pre-agreed per-sheet or per-sq-ft price, so you know the cost upfront.
