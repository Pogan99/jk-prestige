Shingle Roofing FAQ — Jacksonville

Straight answers to what Jacksonville homeowners ask most.

How much does shingle roofing cost in Jacksonville FL?

A standard architectural shingle replacement on a 1,500 sq ft Jacksonville home typically runs $9,000–$14,000 installed. GAF Timberline HDZ runs $10,000–$15,000. Class 4 impact-resistant shingles add $1,500–$3,000 but often reduce homeowner insurance premiums 20–30%, frequently paying back within a few years.

What is the best shingle for Florida's climate?

For Jacksonville, we recommend GAF Timberline HDZ or Owens Corning Duration. Both carry 130 mph wind resistance and current Florida Product Approval. If your home is in a wind-exposed area or you want insurance savings, Class 4 impact-resistant shingles like GAF Armor Shield II are the better long-term investment.

How long do shingles last in Northeast Florida?

Architectural shingles in Jacksonville typically perform 20–25 years with proper attic ventilation. 3-tab shingles often need replacement at 15–18 years due to Florida heat. Overlay installations frequently fail earlier because trapped heat accelerates granule loss.

Do you do overlays or full tear-offs?

Full tear-offs only. Overlays hide deck damage, add structural weight, and void manufacturer warranties. Florida Building Code also limits roofs to two layers — if your home already has two layers, a tear-off is legally required before new installation.

What is a Florida Product Approval (NOA) and why does it matter?

Florida Product Approval certifies that a roofing product meets Florida's high-velocity hurricane zone performance standards. Installing shingles without valid Florida approval can void your homeowner's insurance and result in failed county inspections. Every shingle we install carries current Florida approval.

Can shingles qualify as a cool roof in Florida?

Yes. Several GAF and Owens Corning architectural shingles carry Florida Energy Code cool-roof ratings, reducing attic temperatures 20–30 degrees and lowering cooling costs. We can identify qualifying products for your roof pitch and orientation during the free inspection.
