Sunroom Addition in Jacksonville: Permits, Process, and Cost

A sunroom or Florida room is one of the most popular home additions in Jacksonville — and one that genuinely adds livable square footage, not just aesthetic appeal. In Florida's climate, a well-built sunroom with Low-E glass and HVAC access delivers comfortable year-round living.

JK Prestige manages every phase: permit application with Duval County, foundation pour, framing, glazing, electrical rough-in, and finishes. We don't hand off to a separate enclosure company — our crew builds it from slab to ceiling.

According to the National Association of Realtors, sunroom additions recover 48–70% of their cost at resale in Florida's market. The key is building with permitted, inspected work — cash-basis sunrooms without permits reduce your home's value and complicate your insurance.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does a sunroom addition in Jacksonville require a permit?
Yes — any enclosed or screen room addition requires a Duval County building permit. JK Prestige manages the full permit process.
How much does a sunroom addition cost in Jacksonville FL?
Screened enclosures start around $15,000–$30,000. Glass sunrooms with HVAC run $35,000–$80,000+ depending on size and finishes.
How long does it take to build a sunroom in Jacksonville?
Most sunrooms take 3–6 weeks from permit approval to completion. Larger glass sunrooms with HVAC can take 6–10 weeks.
Can JK Prestige build a sunroom on the back of my Jacksonville home?
Yes — we work with most rear-yard configurations and handle any HOA submittals required.
Does a sunroom addition add value to a Jacksonville home?
Yes — permitted sunrooms add appraised square footage, which directly supports your home's market value. Unpermitted enclosures can actually reduce value and complicate insurance.
