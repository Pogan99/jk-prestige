The 24-Point Roof Inspection Checklist

Every free roof inspection in Jacksonville covers these 24 items across six categories — documented in writing with photographs.

No pressure guarantee: You will receive the written inspection report and photos regardless of whether you schedule any work. We never make you feel obligated. The inspection is genuinely free — it is how we earn the opportunity to work with you if and when the time is right.

Shingles & Field Surface

  • Granule loss and bare spots
  • Cracking, curling, or cupping
  • Missing, lifted, or displaced shingles
  • Hail impact marks and bruising
  • Wind seal strip adhesion

Ridge & Hip

  • Ridge cap condition and fastening
  • Hip shingle alignment and seal
  • Ridge vent operation and obstruction
  • Structural sagging along ridge line

Valleys & Flashing

  • Valley open or woven weave condition
  • Step flashing at walls and dormers
  • Chimney counter and cap flashing
  • Drip edge condition at eaves and rakes

Penetrations

  • Pipe boot condition (rubber collar integrity)
  • Skylight flashing and curb seal
  • HVAC curb flashing
  • Attic fan and exhaust vent seals

Gutters & Drainage

  • Granule accumulation in gutters
  • Gutter pitch and secure fastening
  • Downspout extension and discharge
  • Fascia board moisture damage

Decking & Attic

  • Visible deck soft spots or deflection
  • Attic moisture, mold, or staining
  • Attic ventilation balance (intake vs. exhaust)
  • Insulation condition and coverage

Free Roof Inspection FAQ

Why is the inspection genuinely free? What's the catch?

There is no catch and no obligation. We offer free inspections because homeowners who know their roof's actual condition are better clients when they do need work done — they've already seen our professionalism, our documentation quality, and our approach. If your roof is fine, great. If it needs work, you'll know exactly what and why. We earn the relationship on the inspection; the sale takes care of itself.

How often should a Jacksonville homeowner have their roof inspected?

Annually is the recommendation for any roof over 10 years old, and after every named tropical system or significant hail event regardless of age. Florida's UV intensity and humidity accelerate roofing material degradation faster than most of the country. Many insurance carriers are also now requiring recent inspection reports for policy renewals in coastal Florida counties.

Can I use your inspection report for my insurance or a home sale?

Yes. The written report and timestamped photos are yours to use however you need. For insurance purposes, we format storm damage reports to match the documentation structure insurance adjusters expect. For home sales, buyers and sellers both benefit from a credentialed inspection by a licensed, GAF Master Elite certified contractor rather than a general home inspector whose roofing-specific training may be limited.
