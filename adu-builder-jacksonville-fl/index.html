ADUs in Jacksonville — What You Need to Know

Florida's ADU landscape has evolved significantly in recent years. State legislation passed in 2023 (HB 1573) requires local governments to permit ADUs in any area zoned for single-family residential use, significantly expanding where ADUs can be built in Jacksonville and Duval County. This legislation has removed many of the previous zoning barriers that made ADU construction difficult, but permitting requirements — building, electrical, plumbing, and mechanical — still apply in full.

In Jacksonville, ADU permits are processed by the City of Jacksonville's Building Inspection Division. Applications require site plans showing the ADU's location relative to property lines and existing structures, floor plans, and construction documents meeting Florida Building Code requirements. We prepare and submit complete permit packages on your behalf.

The income potential from a Jacksonville ADU is meaningful. Market rent for a well-built 400-600 square foot ADU in Jacksonville varies by neighborhood but generally ranges from $900 to $1,800 per month. Over a 10-year period, this income can offset a significant portion of the ADU construction cost, making the investment one of the better financial decisions available to homeowners in the current market.

ADU design requires balancing the needs of future occupants with the constraints of the existing lot. Setback requirements, lot coverage limits, maximum ADU size limits, parking requirements, and utility connection logistics all affect what is practically achievable on your specific property. We assess all of these factors during the initial site consultation before any design work begins.

ADUs on lots in flood zones require additional attention. If your Jacksonville property has a flood zone designation, the ADU foundation may need to be elevated, which adds cost but ensures the structure meets insurance and code requirements. We identify flood zone status and its implications during the site visit.

Financing for Jacksonville ADU projects is available through our third-party lending partners. With the income potential of a rented ADU, many financing structures make sense. Ask about available options during your estimate consultation.

Frequently Asked Questions — ADU Builder in Jacksonville FL

Are ADUs legal in Jacksonville FL?

Yes. Florida legislation (HB 1573, 2023) requires municipalities to permit ADUs in single-family zones. Jacksonville's Building Inspection Division processes ADU permits. Setback requirements, lot coverage limits, and utility requirements still apply.

How much does it cost to build an ADU in Jacksonville?

ADU construction costs in Jacksonville range from approximately $80,000 for a basic detached studio to $200,000+ for a fully equipped two-bedroom ADU with a full kitchen and bath. Size, finish level, and utility connection complexity are the primary drivers.

Can I rent out my ADU in Jacksonville?

Yes. A properly permitted ADU can be rented to tenants in Jacksonville. HOA restrictions may apply in some neighborhoods — check your HOA documents if applicable. Short-term rental (Airbnb/VRBO) is subject to Jacksonville's short-term rental ordinance, which varies by zone.

How long does it take to build an ADU in Jacksonville?

Most ADU projects take 4 to 8 months from permit approval depending on size and configuration. Permit review at the City of Jacksonville typically takes 2-6 weeks for a complete application.

What permits are required for a Jacksonville ADU?

A building permit plus subpermits for electrical, plumbing (if kitchen or bath is included), and mechanical are required. We manage all permit applications and inspections.
