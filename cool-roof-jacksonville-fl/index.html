Cool Roofing in Jacksonville: How It Works, What It Saves, and Whether It's Worth It

Florida Building Code does not require cool roofs for most residential projects, but Florida's energy code does incentivize them through compliance pathways that use cool roofing to meet insulation requirements with slightly less attic insulation.

The US Department of Energy's Oak Ridge National Laboratory has found that cool roofs in Florida's climate zone deliver energy savings of $0.05–$0.15 per square foot per year — meaning a 2,000 sq ft roof saves $100–$300 annually in cooling costs.

JK Prestige installs cool roof products as part of standard re-roof projects when requested, and advises clients on the cost-benefit based on their specific situation — roof color preferences, existing attic insulation, and attic ventilation.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a cool roof and why does it matter in Jacksonville FL?
A cool roof uses high Solar Reflectance Index (SRI) materials to reflect solar energy instead of absorbing it. In Jacksonville's climate, this can reduce attic temperatures by 40–60°F and cooling costs by 15–25%.
Does JK Prestige install cool roofs in Jacksonville?
Yes — ENERGY STAR rated reflective shingles, white TPO, and high-SRI metal roofing throughout Jacksonville and Northeast Florida.
How much does a cool roof cost vs. a standard roof in Jacksonville?
Cool roof materials (reflective shingles, white TPO) typically add $500–$2,000 to a standard replacement cost. The energy savings usually pay back the premium in 3–5 years.
Does a cool roof qualify for Florida insurance discounts?
Cool roofs with appropriate wind-load ratings may qualify for wind mitigation credits. TPO systems on commercial buildings can qualify for energy-efficient building credits in some programs.
What is the best cool roofing material for a Jacksonville home?
For residential: ENERGY STAR rated reflective architectural shingles. For commercial: white TPO. For maximum reflectance and longevity: light-colored standing seam metal.
