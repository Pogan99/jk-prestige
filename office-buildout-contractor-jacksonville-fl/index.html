Office Buildout Services — Jacksonville Commercial Market

Tenant Improvement (TI) Buildouts

Office Buildout Process — Jacksonville

Preconstruction — Budget, Schedule & MEP Coordination

Before design is complete, we engage in preconstruction to produce an open-book budget with subcontractor market pricing, a construction schedule with permit review and inspection milestones, and a MEP coordination review identifying conflicts between overhead systems. Identifying an HVAC duct conflict in preconstruction costs one markup on a shop drawing change — identifying it during installation costs three weeks and a coordination RFI cycle.

Permit Submission — Duval County Building Services

We submit the complete permit package — architectural, MEP, and structural drawings where applicable — to Duval County Building Services. We track plan review status through the county's permit portal, respond to reviewer comments within 48 hours, and coordinate any pre-issuance meetings required by the building official. Permit review for standard office TI in Duval County runs 3-5 weeks.

Demolition & Rough-In

On occupied building projects, demo work is sequenced to avoid dust migration and coordinate with building management on after-hours heavy work windows. MEP rough-in — HVAC distribution, electrical conduit, data pathway (empty conduit or wire management), and plumbing where applicable — is completed before framing closes the ceiling zone, and inspections are called promptly to avoid schedule compression.

Framing, Drywall & Ceiling

JK Prestige self-performs metal stud framing, drywall, and taping — eliminating the coordination delay between a separate framing sub and the drywall sub. Acoustic insulation in partitions, STC-rated assemblies at conference rooms, and proper backing for millwork and wall-mounted AV equipment are installed to spec, not field-improvised.

Finishes, Punch-List & CO

Flooring, painting, millwork, doors and hardware, light fixtures, plumbing trim, and IT/AV rough-in are completed and inspected. We conduct an internal punch-list walkthrough with the tenant's representative before calling the final inspection, ensuring the certificate of occupancy is delivered on or before the lease commencement date.

Frequently Asked Questions — Office Buildouts

How long does an office buildout take in Jacksonville FL?

A standard office buildout — demising walls, MEP rough-in, ceiling grid, flooring, and paint — typically runs 8 to 12 weeks for spaces up to 5,000 sq ft. Larger full-floor buildouts (10,000-20,000 sq ft) run 12 to 16 weeks. Phased occupied-building projects extend the schedule to avoid disrupting adjacent tenants.

Who is responsible for pulling permits on a tenant improvement buildout?

The licensed General Contractor pulls all required building, mechanical, electrical, and plumbing permits through Duval County Building Services. The tenant's architect-of-record stamps the drawings; the GC pulls the permits and is listed as the contractor of record. Some landlord leases require the GC to be pre-approved by building management.

What is a typical office buildout cost per square foot in Jacksonville?

Standard office buildout (open plan with private offices, standard finishes) runs approximately $65 to $110 per square foot. High-finish buildouts with glass partition systems and premium flooring run $110 to $160. Law firm and financial services buildouts with heavy millwork can exceed $180 per square foot. These ranges exclude furniture, AV, and IT infrastructure.

Can JK Prestige work in occupied office buildings?

Yes. Occupied-building office buildouts require phased sequencing, dust control barriers, off-hours noisy work, and coordination with building management for elevator access and after-hours HVAC. We have extensive experience delivering occupied-building office buildouts without disrupting adjacent tenants.

Does JK Prestige handle MEP coordination in office buildouts?

Yes. MEP coordination — ensuring HVAC, electrical, plumbing, and fire suppression layout work together before drywall closes — is a core preconstruction service. We identify conflicts between overhead systems early, coordinate with building engineers for HVAC balancing, and manage the sequence so MEP rough-in inspections don't compress the ceiling schedule.
