Local Roofing Considerations for Ponte Vedra

St. Johns County Permits

All re-roofing requires a St. Johns County building permit. We pull permits before any work begins and coordinate the county inspection. You receive copies of all approvals.

Coastal Wind Exposure

Ponte Vedra homes within 1 mile of the ocean are in Florida's highest wind exposure category. We specify 160+ mph rated systems and aluminum fasteners to prevent salt-air corrosion on all coastal installations.

Tile Structural Assessment

Many Ponte Vedra homes have tile roofs. Before any tile replacement, we assess the structural framing to confirm it can carry tile loading — especially important on older homes that may have been modified.

HOA Compliance

Sawgrass, Marsh Landing, and many Ponte Vedra communities have HOA architectural review requirements for roofing changes. We can advise on common HOA approval requirements and provide documentation for your submission.

Wind Mitigation Reporting

We provide wind mitigation inspection reports with every full replacement. St. Johns County homeowners with hip roofs and secondary water barriers qualify for significant insurance premium reductions.

Ponte Vedra Roofing FAQ

How far is JK Prestige from Ponte Vedra Beach?

Our Jacksonville headquarters is approximately 15–25 minutes from most Ponte Vedra Beach and Palm Valley addresses. We serve all of St. Johns County regularly and are familiar with local permit requirements and HOA approval processes.

What is the most common roof type in Ponte Vedra?

Tile roofing is predominant in established Ponte Vedra Beach and Sawgrass communities. Nocatee and newer developments include significant shingle and some metal roofing. We handle all three systems.

Does Ponte Vedra Beach have different building code requirements than Jacksonville?

Ponte Vedra Beach is in St. Johns County, which follows the Florida Building Code. Some coastal areas have additional requirements for wind exposure classification. We are familiar with these requirements and specify products accordingly.

Is aluminum better than steel for Ponte Vedra's coastal environment?

For homes within 1 mile of the ocean or intercoastal waterway, aluminum is strongly preferred. It is naturally corrosion-resistant without protective coating and will not rust in salt-air environments. Galvalume steel is acceptable further inland but requires more frequent maintenance in coastal zones.
