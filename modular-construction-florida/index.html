Modular Construction in Florida: When It Makes Sense and What to Expect

Modular construction in Florida has seen growing adoption in education, healthcare, and commercial markets where speed-to-occupancy and budget control are priorities. Florida's DCA certifies modular buildings to Florida Building Code — they're not manufactured housing and are treated identically to site-built for code purposes.

JK Prestige manages the site work and installation that factory builders don't: site grading, foundation construction, utility connections, and exterior site improvements. The factory delivers the building — we make the site ready and connect everything.

Permanent modular construction (PMC) has become increasingly sophisticated. Modules arrive with interior finishes, mechanical systems, and electrical rough-in complete. JK Prestige connects them to site utilities and completes exterior site work.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does JK Prestige install modular buildings in Florida?
Yes — permanent and temporary modular building installation throughout Florida, including site prep, foundation, and utility connections.
What permits are required for modular construction in Florida?
Florida DCA-approved modular buildings still require local building permits for foundation and utility connections. JK Prestige manages all permits.
How long does modular construction take vs. site-built?
Modular construction typically delivers 20–40% faster than site-built because factory construction and site work happen simultaneously.
How much does modular construction cost in Florida?
Modular buildings typically cost 10–25% less than comparable site-built, excluding site work which is similar for both.
Does JK Prestige install modular classrooms in Florida?
Yes — modular classroom installation including foundation, utility connections, ADA ramp, and exterior site work.
