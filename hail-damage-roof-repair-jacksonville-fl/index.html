Hail Damage FAQ — Jacksonville

How do I know if my roof has hail damage?

Hail damage on shingles appears as circular dark bruise marks or granule loss exposing the asphalt mat. On gutters and downspouts, hail leaves uniform round dings. Hail damage is not always visible from the ground — a professional inspection with photography is the reliable way to document damage for an insurance claim.

Should I call my insurance company or a roofer first?

Call a qualified roofer first. We can inspect your roof, document damage professionally, and tell you whether damage is sufficient to support a claim. Having professional documentation before the adjuster arrives puts you in a much stronger position.

How long do I have to file a hail damage claim in Florida?

Florida Statute 627.70132 requires windstorm and hail claims to be reported within 3 years of the date of loss. However, documentation becomes harder to tie to a specific storm over time. Do not wait.

Will my insurance cover the full cost of hail damage repair?

Coverage depends on your policy type (replacement cost value vs. actual cash value) and your deductible. RCV policies pay full replacement cost after your deductible. ACV policies depreciate the payment based on your roof's age. We explain exactly what your adjuster's estimate means.
