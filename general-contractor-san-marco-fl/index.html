Renovating in San Marco — What Sets It Apart

San Marco is unlike any other Jacksonville neighborhood from a contractor's perspective. The homes are older, smaller, and more architecturally specific than anything in suburban Duval County. The lots are tighter, the neighbors are closer, and the community has a strong sense of architectural identity. Renovating in San Marco well means understanding all of these factors and making decisions that respect them.

Homes in San Marco were built primarily between 1920 and 1960, which means they carry the characteristics of that era of construction: solid wood framing that has been in place for decades, original hardwood floors, plaster walls over wood lath, and older mechanical systems that have often been partially updated over the years. A thorough pre-construction assessment is essential to understand what conditions exist before construction begins. JK Prestige makes this assessment a standard part of every San Marco project.

Permitting in San Marco is through the City of Jacksonville's Building Inspection Division. San Marco falls within the city's historic overlay districts in some areas, which can trigger additional review requirements for certain exterior modifications. We identify applicable historic district requirements during the site visit and ensure all work is appropriately reviewed and approved.

Kitchen and bathroom renovations in San Marco homes present specific opportunities and constraints. Original cabinetry and tile from the 1940s or 1950s may be worth preserving in some cases; in others, a complete replacement with period-appropriate materials creates a better result. We discuss these trade-offs with clients honestly and help them make informed decisions.

Parking and construction access in San Marco requires thoughtful planning. Narrow streets, tight lots, and close neighbors mean job site management — material staging, crew parking, debris removal — must be more carefully coordinated than on suburban sites. JK Prestige has the experience to manage San Marco projects with minimal disruption to the neighborhood.

Frequently Asked Questions — General Contractor in San Marco Jacksonville

Do you renovate older homes in San Marco?

Yes. San Marco is one of our most active renovation markets. We have significant experience with Jacksonville's historic housing stock and understand the specific conditions and requirements that come with older homes.

Does San Marco have historic preservation requirements?

Parts of San Marco fall within Jacksonville's historic district overlay zones, which can require additional review for certain exterior modifications. We identify applicable requirements during the site visit and ensure all work is properly reviewed and approved.

What are the challenges of renovating a 1930s home in San Marco?

Older homes in San Marco may have plaster walls, original hardwood floors, outdated electrical (knob-and-tube or aluminum branch wiring), galvanized plumbing, and undersized HVAC systems. We assess these conditions during pre-construction and incorporate any required updates into the project scope.

Can you add a room to a San Marco home with a small lot?

Yes, though small San Marco lots require careful attention to setback requirements and lot coverage limits. We assess your specific lot's buildable area and design additions that are feasible within the applicable zoning constraints.

What is the typical cost to renovate a San Marco home?

Kitchen renovations run $25,000-$80,000; bathroom renovations $15,000-$45,000; whole-home renovations $80,000-$250,000+. San Marco homes sometimes have additional costs related to addressing older conditions discovered during construction. We build contingency awareness into estimates for older properties.
