Pre-Engineered Steel Buildings

Factory-fabricated metal building systems (AISC-certified manufacturers) for distribution, storage, and light-manufacturing facilities. Faster erection timelines than site-cast tilt-up, with flexible bay spacing, eave heights from 14 to 40 feet, and optional expansion capability. ESFR Sprinkler Coordination

Our Warehouse Construction Process — 5 Steps

Industrial delivery discipline from site control through operational readiness.

Site Analysis & Preconstruction Budget

JK Prestige reviews geotechnical reports, flood zone maps (particularly relevant in Duval County's low-lying industrial areas), utility capacity, FDOT driveway permit requirements, and ESFR/fire protection design basis before establishing the project budget. Site conditions — fill requirements, dewatering, underground utilities — are the biggest source of budget variance on Northeast Florida industrial sites and must be quantified in preconstruction.

Permit Submission & Utility Coordination

Concurrent submission of building, electrical, plumbing, mechanical, fire protection, and SWPPP permits with JEA (Jacksonville Electric Authority) service application. JEA transformer lead times for large industrial services (1,500–4,000A) can run 6–18 months; initiating utility coordination at permit submission, not after CO, is essential for on-time occupancy.

Mass Grading, Site Utilities & Foundation

Mass grading, underground utility installation, retention pond construction (SWPPP monitoring throughout), and foundation system — spread footings, grade beams, tilt-up pour strip, or pier system per the geotechnical engineer's recommendation. JK Prestige maintains NPDES permit compliance throughout earthwork to prevent NOV exposure for the developer.

Structure, Envelope & MEP Rough-In

Steel erection or tilt-up panel placement, roof deck, insulation, roofing membrane (TPO/EPDM per CSI Division 07), exterior skin, dock door framing and headers, ESFR sprinkler main installation, electrical service and distribution, and LED interior lighting rough-in are sequenced against the master pull-plan to compress the structure-to-CO timeline.

Slab Pour, Finishes & CO

High-tolerance interior slab-on-grade, dock leveler installation, concrete dock aprons, office buildout finishes, exterior paving and striping, site lighting, and final fire protection system test. Building, fire, and electrical final inspections are coordinated on the master schedule with permanent power release from JEA to achieve CO on the committed date.

Warehouse Construction FAQ — Jacksonville FL

Common questions from logistics developers, industrial REITs, and build-to-suit tenants.

What is the difference between tilt-up and pre-engineered steel for warehouse construction?

Tilt-up concrete delivers a durable, low-maintenance building envelope ideal for large-footprint distribution facilities. Pre-engineered steel offers faster erection timelines and lower initial cost for smaller warehouses. JK Prestige delivers both systems and assists developers in selecting the right structure based on site conditions, clear height requirements, fire protection design, and long-term maintenance considerations.

What is ESFR sprinkler and why is it required for warehouses?

Early Suppression Fast Response (ESFR) sprinkler systems suppress large commodity fires in high-pile storage without in-rack sprinklers. ESFR systems are required by NFPA 13 and FM Global for warehouses with clear heights above approximately 25 feet. ESFR requirements drive structural bay spacing, roof deck selection, and water service sizing — all preconstruction decisions.

What does warehouse construction cost per square foot in Jacksonville FL?

Warehouse shell construction in Jacksonville typically ranges from $70 to $120/sq ft for a standard distribution facility, including site work, foundation, structure, roofing, dock doors, and basic MEP. Clear heights above 36 feet, ESFR systems, refrigeration rough-in, and office components add cost. JK Prestige provides detailed line-item estimates within 48 hours.

How long does warehouse construction take in Jacksonville FL?

A standard 100,000–500,000 sq ft warehouse in Jacksonville typically delivers in 10–18 months from groundbreaking to CO, depending on building size, structural system, site conditions, and permit timelines. Pre-engineered steel systems can reduce the structural erection timeline by 4–8 weeks compared to tilt-up.
