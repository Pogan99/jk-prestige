New Home Construction in Jacksonville — The JK Prestige Approach

Building a new home on a Jacksonville lot is a multi-stage process that requires coordination across dozens of trades, multiple permit submissions, and careful scheduling to avoid costly delays. The general contractor you choose is the difference between a smooth 12-month build and a chaotic 24-month experience. JK Prestige has built that reputation on transparency, schedule discipline, and direct owner involvement on every project.

Jacksonville's construction market currently operates under the 2020 Florida Building Code, which incorporates stringent energy efficiency and wind resistance requirements. New homes must meet energy performance thresholds for insulation, windows, mechanical equipment, and air sealing. Wind load requirements vary by location — coastal and near-coastal properties in Duval and St. Johns counties face higher design wind speeds. We build to the applicable requirements from the foundation up, which protects your investment and prevents costly remediation at inspection.

Lot selection affects construction cost, schedule, and long-term livability in ways that aren't always obvious before a purchase. Flood zone designation determines whether elevated construction is required and affects the cost of flood insurance. Soil conditions affect foundation design and cost. Easements, setbacks, and tree preservation requirements constrain what can be built and where. We assist clients in evaluating lots before purchase by assessing buildable area, likely permit timelines, and any site conditions that would affect construction cost.

Our pre-construction process for new homes includes a detailed budget review, material specification, and schedule development before any ground is broken. Long lead-time items — custom windows, specialty HVAC equipment, unique millwork — are identified and ordered early to prevent the schedule delays that plague so many custom builds. This discipline is why JK Prestige has a zero change-order dispute record across 500+ projects.

New home construction can be financed through construction-to-permanent loan products available through traditional lenders. Our third-party lending partners can also provide options for clients exploring construction financing. We are familiar with lender draw schedules and can coordinate inspections and documentation as required.

Our New Home Construction Process

Lot Assessment and Pre-Construction Planning

Frequently Asked Questions — New Home Construction in Jacksonville

How much does new home construction cost per square foot in Jacksonville?

New home construction in Jacksonville typically ranges from $200 to $350 per square foot, excluding land. Entry-level custom homes with standard finish packages fall toward the lower end; homes with luxury finishes, custom millwork, and premium mechanical systems fall at the higher end.

How long does new home construction take in Jacksonville?

Most new custom homes in Jacksonville take 12 to 18 months from permit approval to certificate of occupancy. Lot preparation, permit review timelines, and material lead times all affect the schedule. Our pre-construction planning is specifically designed to minimize delays.

What permits are required for new home construction in Jacksonville?

New home construction requires a master building permit along with separate subpermits for electrical, plumbing, mechanical, and any specialty systems. We manage all permit applications and inspection scheduling on your behalf.

Do I need an architect before hiring JK Prestige?

Not necessarily for the initial consultation. We can refer you to architects we trust if you don't already have plans. If you do have architectural plans, bring them to the consultation and we will review them for constructability and prepare an estimate.

Can you build on a waterfront lot in Jacksonville?

Yes. We have experience with waterfront construction in Jacksonville and the surrounding area, including navigating flood zone requirements, setback regulations, and the structural demands of coastal sites.
