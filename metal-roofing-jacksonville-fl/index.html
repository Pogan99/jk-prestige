Metal Roofing Systems We Install

Two fundamentally different systems — we explain both and recommend the right fit for your home, budget, and wind exposure zone.

Standing seam vs. exposed fastener: Standing seam panels connect at hidden clips, with zero exposed fasteners through the metal surface. This is the system that eliminates leak risk at fastener points and allows thermal movement without metal fatigue. Exposed fastener systems (R-panel, corrugated) cost less upfront but require periodic fastener inspection and resealing to prevent moisture intrusion. For Jacksonville's coastal humidity, we generally recommend standing seam for roofs expected to remain in service 40+ years.

Standing Seam Steel (Galvalume)

Galvalume-coated steel panels with 1.5"–2" mechanically seamed ribs and hidden clip attachment. Wind rated 160–180 mph with Florida NOA. Lifespan 40–60 years. Available in 30+ Kynar finish colors with fade warranty.

Standing Seam Aluminum

Lighter weight than steel, naturally corrosion-resistant — the preferred choice for homes within 1 mile of the ocean or intercoastal waterway. 50–70 year lifespan. Higher upfront cost than Galvalume but superior in salt-air environments.

Exposed Fastener Metal Panels

R-panel and corrugated steel systems. Lower installed cost ($12,000–$18,000 for 1,500 sqft) than standing seam. Well-suited for outbuildings, garages, and budget-conscious residential projects. Requires 5-year fastener inspection schedule.

Metal Roof Repair & Reseal

Fastener replacement, panel seam resealing, ridge cap repair, and flashing repair on existing metal roofs. We identify and repair the actual source of leaks, not just apply roof sealant over symptoms.

Metal Roof Installation Process

Precision fabrication and installation is what separates a 50-year metal roof from one that fails in 15.

Inspection & System Selection

We assess your roof pitch, wind exposure zone, attic structure, and aesthetic goals. Standing seam requires a minimum 3:12 pitch; lower slopes use specific low-slope metal systems. Written recommendation with itemized pricing.

Tear-Off & Deck Preparation

Full removal of existing roofing material. Deck inspected, damaged boards replaced. Proper vapor barrier installed to prevent condensation under metal panels — critical in Florida's humidity.

Panel Fabrication or Delivery

Standing seam panels are often roll-formed on-site for precise fit with zero seams at critical points. Exposed fastener panels are cut to length. All panels measured to within 1/8" of design spec.

Installation & Clip Attachment

Hidden clips installed on proper centers per Florida NOA requirements. Panels snapped and seamed in sequence. Ridge cap, hip caps, and all flashings sealed with Kynar-compatible sealant.

Wind Mitigation Report & Warranty

Wind mitigation inspection report completed and provided at job completion. Manufacturer warranty registered in your name. Insurance submission package prepared on request.

Metal Roofing FAQ — Jacksonville

How much does a metal roof cost in Jacksonville FL?

Exposed fastener metal roofing runs $12,000–$18,000 installed on a 1,500 sq ft home. Standing seam runs $18,000–$32,000 for the same size. Aluminum systems cost 10–15% more than Galvalume steel but are better for coastal salt-air environments within 1 mile of water.

What is the difference between standing seam and exposed fastener?

Standing seam panels lock together at hidden clips, with no exposed fasteners penetrating the metal. This eliminates the primary source of metal roof leaks and allows thermal expansion without metal fatigue. Exposed fastener systems cost less but require periodic fastener inspection and re-torquing.

What wind rating does metal roofing have in Florida?

Properly installed standing seam metal systems carry Florida Product Approval for 160–180 mph wind speeds, exceeding requirements for all Florida wind zones including coastal Duval County. All metal systems we install carry current Florida NOA approval.

How long does a metal roof last in Jacksonville?

Galvalume steel standing seam roofs typically last 40–60 years in Northeast Florida. Aluminum systems last 50–70 years. Both significantly outperform asphalt shingles (20–25 years) in the Florida climate.

Does metal roofing lower homeowner insurance in Florida?

Yes. Metal roofs rated for 160+ mph winds often qualify for wind mitigation credits reducing homeowner insurance premiums 20–40% in Duval County. We provide a wind mitigation inspection report with every metal roof installation for immediate insurer submission.

Is metal roofing noisier than shingles in Florida rain?

Not significantly when installed over solid decking with underlayment. The sound difference between metal and shingles in a properly constructed home is minimal. Noise concerns mainly apply to open-framing installations without decking, which is uncommon in Jacksonville residential construction.
