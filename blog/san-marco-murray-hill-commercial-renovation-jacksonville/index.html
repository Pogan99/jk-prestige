Commercial Renovation in San Marco and Murray Hill: What Business Owners Need to Know

June 5, 2026 · 6 min read · JK Prestige Constructor Corp

San Marco and Murray Hill are where Jacksonville's independent business culture lives. San Marco Square and the surrounding blocks host restaurants, boutiques, and professional offices in buildings that go back to the 1930s. Murray Hill's commercial corridor — primarily along Edgewood Avenue — is in the middle of a decade-long revival. Vacancy rates in both neighborhoods are low, and tenant turnover means regular renovation cycles.

Commercial renovation in both neighborhoods is not a simple permit and start. Older commercial buildings have specific structural and code upgrade requirements that most business owners don't know about until they're in contract. This guide covers what to expect.

Why Older Commercial Buildings Require More Than Standard Permits

Most commercial buildings in San Marco and Murray Hill were constructed before Florida adopted modern building codes. When a tenant improvement or renovation triggers significant work, code compliance upgrades are often required as a condition of permit approval.

The most common triggers:

Change of occupancy — Moving a space from retail to restaurant, from office to medical, or from storage to any occupied use is a change of occupancy that requires a full code review. ADA path of travel updates, restroom upgrades, and egress improvements are typically required.

Over 50% renovation rule — Florida Building Code Section 705.1.1 generally requires accessibility path-of-travel upgrades when the renovation cost exceeds 50% of the structure's replacement value. For older buildings in San Marco and Murray Hill, this threshold is hit more often than owners expect.

Structural load changes — Adding mezzanine storage, second-floor offices, or heavy restaurant equipment (walk-in coolers, commercial hoods) requires structural review and often supplemental steel.

Electrical service — Most older commercial buildings in these neighborhoods are single-phase or underpowered for modern restaurant or medical uses. Panel upgrades are common scope additions.

The Permit Process for Commercial Work in Jacksonville

Commercial permits in Jacksonville run through the City of Jacksonville Building Inspection Division, not Duval County Building Services (which handles unincorporated areas). Downtown, San Marco, and most of Murray Hill fall within city jurisdiction.

The commercial permit review cycle for tenant improvements in these neighborhoods typically runs:

  • Simple fit-out (cosmetic, no structural or MEP changes): 2-4 weeks
  • Moderate renovation (MEP changes, ADA upgrades): 4-8 weeks
  • Change of occupancy or restaurant conversion: 6-12 weeks (fire marshal review required)

Fire Prevention Division review is required for any change of occupancy and any food service operation. Budget for their review timeline.

San Marco Historic Considerations

Parts of the San Marco commercial district fall within the San Marco historic overlay. Exterior modifications — storefront changes, signage, facade work — require review through the Jacksonville Historic Preservation Division before permits are issued.

This doesn't apply to interior renovation. A full gut renovation of the interior of a San Marco building proceeds under standard commercial permits regardless of historic status.

If your project includes exterior work in the historic overlay, plan for an additional 30 to 45 days in the pre-construction timeline.
