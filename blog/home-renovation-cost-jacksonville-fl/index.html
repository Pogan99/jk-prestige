How Much Does a Home Renovation Cost in Jacksonville FL? Real Ranges by Scope

May 4, 2026 · 7 min read · JK Prestige Constructor Corp

The most common question renovation contractors get in Jacksonville is "how much will this cost?" The honest answer is: it depends on what you're actually doing. The problem is that most homeowners don't know how to define "what they're actually doing" precisely enough to get a meaningful number.

This post gives you real cost ranges by scope — not vague ballpark figures, but the numbers JK Prestige Constructor sees on actual Jacksonville projects in 2025. It also explains what drives the final number and why getting a quote without a scope document is not a quote — it's a guess.

---

Scope 1: Cosmetic Refresh — $15,000 to $40,000

A cosmetic refresh doesn't touch the structure, the plumbing, or the electrical. It covers surface-level improvements: interior repaint (walls, trim, ceilings), flooring replacement (LVP, carpet, refinishing existing hardwood), cabinet refinishing or door replacement (without moving boxes), light fixture and hardware replacement, and bathroom fixture swaps (same location, no rough-in work).

At $15,000, you're painting a medium-sized house and replacing flooring in the main living areas. At $40,000, you're doing a complete interior repaint, flooring throughout, cabinet door replacement in kitchen and bathrooms, new fixtures, and upgraded hardware.

What drives the range: square footage, finish level (builder-grade LVP vs. engineered hardwood), and whether any surfaces need repair before the cosmetic work goes on.

What it doesn't include: anything that requires a permit, any structural work, any plumbing relocation, any electrical panel upgrade.

---

Scope 2: Kitchen Remodel Without Moving Walls — $40,000 to $90,000

A kitchen remodel that keeps the layout largely intact — same footprint, same rough-in locations — is fundamentally a surface and systems replacement: cabinets, countertops, appliances, backsplash, flooring, and lighting fixtures.

At $40,000, you're doing stock cabinets from a home center, laminate or budget quartz countertops, new appliances in the mid-tier range, and basic lighting. At $90,000, you're doing semi-custom cabinetry, engineered stone countertops, professional-grade appliances (Sub-Zero, Wolf, or equivalent), custom backsplash tile, updated lighting layout, and upgraded flooring.

What drives the range: cabinet quality and configuration (stock vs. semi-custom vs. custom), countertop material (laminate at $20-$35/sqft vs. premium quartz or marble at $80-$150/sqft), appliance brand tier, and finish material selections across the board.

What it includes beyond surfaces: in a kitchen of any age in Jacksonville, budget for a rough-in inspection and likely some electrical upgrade work (GFCIs required within six feet of the sink, dedicated circuits for large appliances). Don't assume the existing wiring will pass inspection when the permit is pulled.

---

Scope 3: Full Kitchen Gut-Reno — $80,000 to $180,000+

A gut-reno takes the kitchen to the studs, fully relocates plumbing and electrical to the new layout, and potentially moves walls. This is the appropriate approach when the existing layout doesn't work, when the existing rough-in is too dated or inadequate to support the planned appliances, or when the scope of finish upgrades makes it more efficient to start clean.

At $80,000 in Jacksonville in 2025, you're getting a functional, well-finished kitchen gut-reno with semi-custom cabinetry and mid-range finishes. At $150,000 to $180,000+, you're getting custom cabinetry, premium stone, high-end appliances, custom hood fabrication, engineered hardwood or specialty tile, a relocated island or structural opening, and a kitchen that photographs like a magazine spread.

A full kitchen gut-reno requires a permit in Duval County. Permit fees on this scope range from $300 to $800 depending on the included trades. Plan review typically takes two to four weeks.

---

Scope 4: Bathroom Gut-Reno — $25,000 to $65,000

A single bathroom gut-reno at standard scale (60 to 100 square feet) runs $25,000 to $40,000 in Jacksonville at mid-range finishes: new tile throughout, new vanity with stone top, new toilet and fixtures, frameless glass shower enclosure, new lighting, and updated ventilation.

At $40,000 to $65,000, you're doing a primary bathroom with a larger footprint, heated tile floors, a free-standing tub in addition to a walk-in shower, custom vanity, premium stone, and a high-end plumbing fixture package.

What pushes the number up: large-format tile (higher labor cost per square foot), heated floor systems (materials plus electrical rough-in), custom glass enclosures, and premium fixture brands.

---

Scope 5: Full Home Gut-Reno — $150 to $300 per Square Foot

A full gut-reno is the most variable scope. At $150/sqft in Jacksonville, you're doing a complete interior renovation on a house with reasonable bones: new flooring throughout, new kitchen, all bathrooms renovated, repainted, mechanical systems serviced or replaced as needed, and new interior doors and trim. This is a livable, updated result but not a luxury product.

At $250 to $300/sqft, you're going deeper: new windows, full HVAC replacement, full electrical panel upgrade and rewire, full plumbing repiping, custom millwork throughout, luxury finish levels in kitchen and bathrooms, and smart home systems integration.

The difference between $150 and $300/sqft is not cosmetic — it's whether the major systems (HVAC, electrical, plumbing, windows, roof) are being replaced alongside the finishes. A 2,500-sqft house at $150/sqft is a $375,000 renovation. At $300/sqft, it's a $750,000 renovation. These are not interchangeable.

---

Scope 6: Addition — $200 to $350 per Square Foot

A bedroom or living space addition in Jacksonville runs $200 to $275/sqft for standard construction. A premium addition with a full bathroom, vaulted ceilings, custom finishes, and seamless integration with the existing structure runs $275 to $350/sqft.

What drives the addition cost significantly higher than interior renovation: a new foundation, new exterior walls (framing, exterior cladding, windows, insulation, drywall), a new roof section, and MEP extension from the existing house. Every one of these items has a Florida building permit requirement and an inspection sequence.

---

What Drives Jacksonville Renovation Costs in 2025

Labor Market

Jacksonville's construction labor market remains tight. Skilled trades — finish carpentry, tile setting, cabinetry installation, custom millwork work — command a premium. Labor is typically 40 to 60% of total renovation cost on work-intensive scopes.

Material Lead Times

Most standard finish materials are readily available. Custom cabinetry still runs 10 to 16 weeks from order. Custom tile from European suppliers runs 8 to 14 weeks. Specialty plumbing fixtures can run 6 to 20 weeks. A renovation that doesn't account for lead times in the schedule will have crews standing by waiting for materials.

Permit Fees in Duval County

Duval County building permit fees are calculated as a percentage of construction value. For a $200,000 renovation, permit fees typically run $800 to $2,000 depending on included trades. Plan review for complex scopes adds two to four weeks before ground breaks.

Finish Level

The single biggest cost variable in any renovation scope is finish level. The difference between mid-range and high-end finishes in a kitchen gut-reno alone can be $40,000 to $60,000 on the same footprint with the same labor.

---

Why Getting a Ballpark Without a Scope Document Is Meaningless

A GC who gives you a "ballpark" renovation cost over the phone without a scope document is giving you a number calculated to keep you engaged — not a number you can rely on. The specific selections, structural conditions, existing mechanical state, and finish level determine the actual cost. Without defining all of those items in a written scope document, there is no price to quote.

The correct process: site walk, existing conditions survey, scope document development, written selections, then fixed-price estimate. That process produces a number you can build a budget around.
