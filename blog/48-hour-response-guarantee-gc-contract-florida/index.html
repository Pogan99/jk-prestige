The 48-Hour Response Guarantee: Why It Should Be in Every GC Contract in Florida

May 4, 2026 · 7 min read · JK Prestige Constructor Corp

---

The most common complaint Jacksonville homeowners have about their general contractor is not the work — it's the silence. A question about a budget line gets a voicemail. An RFI about a schedule delay gets a "we're working on it." A change request sits unanswered for ten days while work continues around it. By the time you get a real answer, three more questions have piled up and you're not sure what's actually happening on your project.

A 48-hour response guarantee written into the construction contract is the only contractual remedy for this. It means any owner question — budget, schedule, RFI, change request — receives a written reply within 48 hours. Not a call saying they'll look into it. A written response with an answer or a documented timeline for one. JK Prestige Constructor includes this guarantee in every contract. This post explains what it covers, why most GCs don't offer it, and how to verify whether a contractor actually honors it before you sign.

---

What a 48-Hour Response Guarantee Actually Covers

The guarantee is not a catch-all for any communication. It applies to four specific categories of owner inquiry:

1. RFI Responses (Requests for Information)

An RFI is a formal question about the construction drawings, specifications, or scope — typically raised when field conditions differ from the drawings, or when a design detail is ambiguous. On a residential project, this might be a question about where the electrical panel will land now that the structural wall was moved, or what finish trim profile was specified for the window casings.

An unanswered RFI stops work. If the framing crew can't proceed without knowing where the panel goes, the clock runs until someone answers. A 48-hour RFI response commitment means the owner gets a written answer — not "we're waiting on the architect" — within 48 hours. If the answer depends on a third party, the GC's response documents that dependency, who they've contacted, and when they expect to have the answer.

2. Budget Questions

Mid-project budget questions are where homeowners most often feel left in the dark. What does the current spend look like? Is the contingency line intact? If a potential change order is pending, what is the exposure?

JK Prestige provides weekly written owner reports that include budget variance against the fixed-price contract. But when a homeowner has a specific budget question outside the weekly report cycle, the 48-hour guarantee means they get a written answer — current line-item spend, contingency status, open change requests — within two business days.

3. Schedule Updates

When a homeowner asks "will we make the finish date?" they deserve a direct answer backed by the current schedule, not a reassurance. Under the 48-hour guarantee, a schedule question gets a written response: current schedule status, any milestone changes, and the reason for any variance.

On JK Prestige projects over $2M, the Last Planner System (LPS) tracks Percent Plan Complete (PPC) — the percentage of weekly commitments that were actually met — so a schedule question can be answered with data, not impression.

4. Change Requests

When a homeowner requests a scope change — adding a room, changing a finish, modifying a structural element — the 48-hour guarantee means the GC responds in writing within 48 hours with one of three things: a written change order with a fixed price, a request for additional information needed to price it, or a statement that the change conflicts with the existing design and what would need to change to make it work.

What does not count as a response: "we'll look into it," "we have to talk to the electrician," or any reply that defers the issue without a documented next step and timeline.

Why Most GCs Don't Offer a 48-Hour Response Guarantee

The reason is organizational, not motivational. Most GCs of any size are managing multiple projects simultaneously, and their communication runs through coordinators — a project manager's assistant, a scheduling coordinator, an office manager who takes calls when the PM is in the field. The coordinator logs the question, forwards it to the right person, and waits for a response that comes back through the same chain.

A 48-hour guarantee requires that the person with the answer is accessible and accountable. At most GCs, that person — the project manager, the estimator, the superintendent — is managing four to eight jobs at once and is not structured to respond to any single owner within 48 hours.

At JK Prestige, the accountability structure is different because the operating model is different. The principal who signed the contract is the person with the answer — on budget, on schedule, on RFIs. There is no coordinator chain between the owner's question and the person responsible for the answer. The principal's direct number is written into the contract. The 48-hour guarantee is something JK can make because it is structured to honor it.

How to Verify Whether a Contractor Actually Honors the Guarantee

A 48-hour response guarantee in a contract is a legal commitment. Verifying that a contractor has honored it on past projects requires talking to past clients — specifically, clients who had problems during the build.

Ask for References Who Had Issues

Every GC will give you references who are happy with the finished product. Ask specifically for references from projects where something went wrong mid-construction — a material delay, a subcontractor problem, a design conflict — and ask those references:

  • When you raised the issue, how long did it take to get a substantive written response?
  • Did the GC's answer actually resolve the question, or did it defer to a later date?
  • If you called the principal directly, did they answer or call back within the same day?
  • Did budget questions get answered with specific numbers, or with reassurances?

A GC who consistently honored a 48-hour response guarantee will have references who can describe specific examples of timely, written communication during a difficult moment in the project. A GC who did not honor it will produce references who either say communication was "fine" in vague terms, or who describe the frustration of being ignored mid-project.

Request a Sample Weekly Owner Report

Ask the GC to show you the format of their weekly owner report. A real weekly owner report — not a template — will include:

  • Budget variance (actual spend vs. fixed-price contract, line by line)
  • Schedule status (current milestone vs. baseline schedule)
  • Open RFIs (question, date raised, date answered, status)
  • Look-ahead schedule (what work is planned in the next two weeks)
  • Open change requests (scope description, price, status)

If the GC can't show you a real example from a completed project, the weekly report is aspirational, not operational.

Look at the Contract Language

The 48-hour guarantee should be specific in the contract. "Timely communication" is not a guarantee. The contract language should state: any written owner inquiry regarding budget, schedule, RFI status, or change request scope will receive a written response within 48 hours. If no answer is available within 48 hours, the GC will provide a written interim response documenting the status and a timeline for the full answer.
