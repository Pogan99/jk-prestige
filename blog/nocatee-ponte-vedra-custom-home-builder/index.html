Nocatee and Ponte Vedra Custom Home Builder: What to Expect From Ground-Up Construction

June 5, 2026 · 8 min read · JK Prestige Constructor Corp

Nocatee and Ponte Vedra Beach are where Jacksonville's custom home market concentrates. St. Johns County has posted the highest growth numbers in Florida for four consecutive years. New custom homes in Nocatee start around 2,500 sqft and regularly run past 5,000. Ponte Vedra Beach resales often get demolished and replaced with new custom builds on the existing lot.

Both markets have a shared problem: the demand for quality custom GCs far outpaces the supply of GCs who actually self-perform the critical trades, hold fixed-price contracts, and put a principal's name on the project.

JK Prestige is based in Jacksonville and builds ground-up in both markets. This guide explains what custom home construction looks like in Nocatee and Ponte Vedra, what the St. Johns County permit process requires, and what separates the GCs who deliver from the ones who don't.

St. Johns County vs. Duval County: What's Different

Nocatee sits in St. Johns County. Ponte Vedra Beach also falls in St. Johns County jurisdiction, despite its proximity to Jacksonville. This matters for permitting and inspections.

Key differences for builders:

  • St. Johns County Building Services processes permits separately from Duval County. Permit applications are submitted through their online portal; inspection scheduling runs through the county's system.
  • HOA overlay — Both Nocatee (master-planned by TPIF) and many Ponte Vedra communities have architectural review boards that must approve plans before permits are submitted. Design submittals to the ARB happen before you break ground.
  • Flood zone considerations — Parts of Ponte Vedra Beach are in FEMA flood zones requiring elevated foundations and specific construction methods. The survey and flood determination happen during pre-construction.
  • Impact fees — St. Johns County impact fees for new residential construction are among the higher in the state. These are calculated at permit issuance and are not part of the GC's contract — they're an owner cost.

JK Prestige builds in both counties. We know the inspection process and the ARB requirements in the communities we work in regularly.

What Custom Home Construction Costs in Nocatee and Ponte Vedra

Custom home cost in 2026 is not a single number. It's a range defined by finish level, site conditions, and design complexity.

Custom home (2,500-3,500 sqft, mid-high finish, standard lot): $450 to $550 per sqft of finished area, or $1.1M to $1.9M total.

Custom home (4,000-5,500 sqft, high finish, pool and outdoor living): $550 to $700 per sqft, or $2.2M to $3.85M total. Outdoor kitchen, pool, screen enclosure, and landscape are typically separate contracts.

Lot improvements — Clearing, grading, fill, and drainage are not always included in "per sqft" pricing. Ask every GC whether site work and utilities are in their number or excluded.

What drives cost up fast in Nocatee and Ponte Vedra:

  • Elevated foundation requirements in flood zones
  • Hurricane impact glazing (required for new construction in St. Johns County)
  • Custom millwork — beams, built-ins, coffered ceilings
  • Generator and whole-home surge systems (standard expectation in this market)

The Ground-Up Construction Timeline in St. Johns County

For a 3,500 sqft custom home in Nocatee, this is a realistic sequence:

  1. ARB design review and approval: 4-8 weeks
  2. Permit application and approval (St. Johns County): 6-10 weeks
  3. Site work and foundation: 4-6 weeks
  4. Framing: 4-6 weeks
  5. Roof sheathing and dry-in: 2-3 weeks
  6. Rough MEP (mechanical, electrical, plumbing): 4-6 weeks
  7. Insulation and drywall: 3-5 weeks
  8. Exterior finish and windows: 3-4 weeks
  9. Interior finishes, cabinetry, flooring: 8-12 weeks
  10. Fixtures, trim, punch list: 3-4 weeks

Total from ARB submission to CO: 13 to 18 months. Any GC promising under 12 months for a ground-up custom home in this market is not accounting for permit timing or the reality of today's trade schedules.

Self-Perform vs. Full-Broker: Why It Matters in Custom Home Construction

Most residential GCs in the Jacksonville market broker everything. They're project managers who hire framing crews, drywall crews, roofing crews, and concrete crews independently. When a trade falls behind or produces poor work, the GC is one step removed from fixing it.

JK Prestige self-performs framing, drywall, concrete, and roofing. On a custom home build, that covers roughly 40% to 55% of the total construction cost. When our own crew is running the framing, we control the quality and the pace. We see the RFIs before they become schedule problems. We don't wait for a sub to respond to an email.

For Nocatee and Ponte Vedra buyers spending $1M to $4M on a custom home, knowing the GC controls the most critical trades is not a minor detail.

Fixed-Price Contracts for Custom Homes: How This Works

Custom home construction can run as fixed-price, cost-plus, or GMP (Guaranteed Maximum Price). JK Prestige offers fixed-price contracts on custom homes where the design is complete or substantially complete at contract time.

What fixed-price means: the number in the contract is the number you pay, assuming no owner-directed scope changes. Material escalation, labor surprises, subcontractor scheduling issues — those are the GC's problem, not yours.

What fixed-price requires: a complete set of construction documents before signing. If the design is still evolving when you want to break ground, a GMP contract with a defined contingency is more honest than a fixed-price number built on assumptions.

Either way, you get a written scope, a written price, and a principal's name on the contract.
