Gut Renovation Cost Breakdown by Room in Jacksonville, FL (2025)

June 6, 2026 · 7 min read · JK Prestige Constructor Corp

Most Jacksonville homeowners searching for renovation cost information find either vague national averages that don't match the local market or contractor quotes that appear from nowhere. This is JK's attempt to give you real numbers for the Jacksonville market in 2025, with the caveats that matter. These are not low-bid numbers. They are what a licensed, OSHA 30, self-perform GC actually charges for gut renovation in Northeast Florida.

What "Gut Renovation" Means in This Breakdown

A gut renovation goes down to the studs (or concrete/block in some cases): all finishes removed, all fixtures removed, all tile removed. What remains is the structural shell — framing, slab, and exterior walls. The renovation starts fresh from that point: new MEP rough-in where needed, new insulation, new drywall, new finishes throughout.

Gut is not cosmetic. Gut is not "replacing the kitchen counters and painting." Gut means the room is a box before it is rebuilt.

Kitchen: $35,000-95,000

The kitchen has the highest cost concentration of any room in a gut renovation because it combines tile, cabinetry, countertops, appliances, plumbing, electrical, and HVAC in roughly 150-300 sqft.

What Drives Cost in a Kitchen Gut

  • Cabinet quality: Stock cabinets from a big box store: $4,000-10,000 installed. Semi-custom from a cabinet shop: $12,000-25,000. Full custom: $25,000-60,000+.
  • Countertops: Laminate: $40-80/linear foot. Quartz: $80-140/linear foot. Natural stone: $100-200+/linear foot.
  • Appliances: Builder-grade package: $3,500-6,000. Mid-range: $8,000-18,000. High-end: $20,000-50,000+.
  • Tile work: Floor and backsplash tile at $10-15/sqft installed for standard porcelain; $25-50/sqft for natural stone or large format.
  • Layout changes: Moving the sink or range to a new location requires new drain rough-in or gas line relocation — add $3,000-8,000.

JK gut kitchen, 200 sqft, semi-custom cabinets, quartz counters, mid-grade appliances: $48,000-65,000.

Primary Bathroom: $25,000-70,000

Second-highest cost concentration. Tile, plumbing fixtures, custom vanity, and waterproofing all combine in 100-180 sqft.

  • Tile throughout (floor, shower walls, tub surround): 300-500 sqft total. Standard porcelain at $8-12/sqft installed: $2,400-6,000. Large-format or natural stone: $12,000-25,000.
  • Shower system: Prefab insert: $1,500-3,500. Tile shower with glass door: $8,000-15,000. Walk-in with frameless glass: $12,000-22,000.
  • Vanity and fixtures: Stock double vanity with standard faucets: $2,500-5,000 installed. Custom millwork with designer fixtures: $10,000-25,000.
  • Freestanding tub (if added): $3,500-15,000 depending on material, plus $2,000-4,000 for plumbing rough-in modification.

JK gut primary bath, 150 sqft, tile shower, semi-custom vanity, mid-range fixtures: $38,000-55,000.

Secondary Bathrooms: $14,000-35,000 Each

Secondary baths run smaller and simpler. 60-100 sqft typical in Jacksonville residential.

  • Same trades involved (tile, plumbing, electrical) but smaller quantities
  • Tub/shower combo instead of separate soaking tub and walk-in shower
  • Single vanity instead of double

JK gut secondary bath, 80 sqft, tub/shower tile, single vanity: $18,000-28,000.

Half Bath / Powder Room: $8,000-18,000

  • No shower or tub — just toilet, vanity, and flooring
  • Smaller tile quantity; vanity is a statement piece in many powder rooms
  • Custom fixtures or vessel sink add cost quickly

Living Room / Family Room: $15,000-35,000

Gut renovation of a living area involves framing (if wall configuration changes), drywall, finish carpentry, and flooring. No plumbing. MEP scope is limited to electrical and potentially HVAC register relocation.

  • Flooring: Hardwood at $8-14/sqft installed. LVP at $4-8/sqft installed. Tile at $8-12/sqft installed.
  • Trim work: Crown molding, base, casings — $3-7/sqft of room perimeter for quality installation
  • Built-in shelving or entertainment center: $5,000-20,000 depending on complexity
  • Fireplace surround (if existing fireplace): $3,000-12,000 depending on material

JK gut living room, 400 sqft, hardwood floors, trim, no structural changes: $18,000-30,000.

Bedrooms: $10,000-22,000 Each

Bedrooms are the most straightforward gut renovation rooms. No plumbing, minimal MEP changes, no tile.

  • Flooring: $8-14/sqft for hardwood; $4-8/sqft for LVP
  • Closet build-out: Stock closet system: $1,500-4,000. Custom built-in: $4,000-12,000.
  • Trim: Base, casing, crown — $2,000-5,000 per room for full trim package

JK gut bedroom, 200 sqft, hardwood floors, full trim, basic closet: $11,000-18,000.

Structural Changes: Price Them Separately

Any structural change — load-bearing wall removal, new opening in an exterior wall, beam installation, floor leveling — is priced separately from the room finish gut. These items are:

  • Load-bearing wall removal (including beam): $4,000-12,000 depending on beam span and load path complexity
  • New exterior window or door opening: $3,000-8,000
  • Floor leveling (slab topping or subfloor work): $3-8/sqft
  • Structural floor system repair (rot, damaged joists): $5,000-20,000+ depending on extent

These items are identified in JK's pre-construction existing conditions assessment and included in the fixed-price contract before work starts.
