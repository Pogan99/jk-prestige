How to Verify a Roofing Contractor's Manufacturer Certification

May 4, 2026 · 7 min read · JK Prestige Constructor Corp

Every roofing contractor in Jacksonville will tell you they are certified. After a hurricane, that claim multiplies. Storm chasers pull into neighborhoods within 48 hours of a storm event, hand out business cards, and mention manufacturer programs by name. Some of them are actually certified. Most are not.

Manufacturer certification is verifiable. It takes less than five minutes to confirm, and the difference between a certified contractor and an uncertified one is not just a sticker — it is whether the manufacturer warranty on your new roof is worth anything. This guide walks you through exactly how to check.

1. Understand What Manufacturer Certification Actually Means

A roofing manufacturer warranty has two components: the materials warranty covering defective shingles, and the workmanship warranty covering installation errors. The materials warranty transfers automatically to any homeowner who buys the product. The workmanship warranty does not.

Only contractors who have earned and maintained manufacturer certification are eligible to register workmanship warranties on behalf of homeowners. If your roofer is not certified, the manufacturer's workmanship warranty is unavailable to you regardless of what the marketing language on the shingle packaging says.

Certification programs require contractors to demonstrate minimum years of experience, maintain valid licensing and insurance, pass inspections, and in some programs, meet annual installation volume minimums. They are not pay-to-play programs — a contractor cannot simply buy certification. The manufacturer audits eligibility.

This distinction matters in Florida, where wind damage, impact damage from hail, and granule loss from UV exposure are the most common warranty claims. Without workmanship coverage, a repair claim that should be the contractor's problem becomes the homeowner's.

2. Know the Major Certification Programs

Four programs cover the majority of residential roofing in Jacksonville.

GAF Master Elite is the top tier of GAF's contractor certification program. Fewer than 3% of roofing contractors in the country qualify. Requirements include a minimum of three years in business, valid license, adequate insurance, a satisfactory rating with the Better Business Bureau (BBB), and a commitment to ongoing training. Master Elite contractors can offer GAF's Golden Pledge Limited Warranty, which is GAF's non-prorated, non-dollar-limited workmanship warranty — the most comprehensive warranty available in the GAF system.

GAF Certified Contractor is the entry-level GAF certification. It unlocks the System Plus Limited Warranty but not the Golden Pledge. Requirements are lower than Master Elite, and the pool of certified contractors is larger. Verify the tier — "GAF Certified" and "GAF Master Elite" are not interchangeable.

CertainTeed SELECT ShingleMaster is CertainTeed's highest contractor designation. SELECT ShingleMasters can offer the CertainTeed SureStart PLUS warranty, which includes five years of installation workmanship coverage. CertainTeed also has a ShingleMaster designation at a lower tier. As with GAF, confirm which level you are dealing with.

Owens Corning Preferred Contractor is the mid-tier Owens Corning designation. The Platinum Preferred Contractor designation is the top tier and unlocks the Owens Corning Platinum Protection Limited Warranty with extended workmanship coverage.

When a contractor tells you they are "manufacturer certified," ask them which program and which tier. Those are two different questions, and vague answers to either one are a warning sign.

3. Verify GAF Certification at gaf.com

GAF maintains a public contractor locator at gaf.com/roofing-contractors. The search returns contractors by zip code and displays their certification tier — Certified Contractor vs. Master Elite — along with their business name and location.

Search for the contractor by business name or browse results in the 32218, 32225, 32256, and other Jacksonville-area zip codes where they claim to work. If the contractor's business name does not appear in the results, they are not currently GAF-certified.

Two things to check: first, confirm the business name matches exactly what the contractor's license lists. Some contractors operate under DBAs (doing business as names) that differ from their licensed entity. Second, certifications can lapse. A contractor who was Master Elite in 2021 may not be in 2025. The locator reflects current certification status.

Screenshot the results page with the date visible. If a dispute arises later, that documentation establishes their certification status at the time of contracting.

4. Verify CertainTeed Certification at certainteed.com

CertainTeed's contractor locator is at certainteed.com/find-a-contractor. Filter by roofing and your zip code. Results indicate whether the contractor holds ShingleMaster or SELECT ShingleMaster status.

The same verification discipline applies: confirm the business name, confirm the tier, note the date. CertainTeed's locator is updated regularly but not in real time. If a contractor's certification status has changed very recently, call CertainTeed's contractor services line to confirm current standing.

For Owens Corning, use owenscorning.com/roofing/contractors to search by location. The results distinguish between Preferred and Platinum Preferred status.

None of these lookups require an account. They are public tools designed specifically so homeowners can verify the contractors approaching them after a storm.

5. Ask for the Certification Letter, Not a Truck Sticker

Truck decals, magnetic signs, and laminated cards in a contractor's truck are not certifications. They are marketing materials. Any contractor can print a GAF logo on a business card.

The actual certification letter is issued by the manufacturer and addressed to the contractor's business entity. It states the certification tier, the effective date, and any conditions. Ask to see it. A legitimately certified contractor has this document and will provide it without hesitation.

If the contractor cannot produce a current certification letter, treat the verbal claim as unverified and confirm through the manufacturer's online locator before signing anything.

6. Understand What Each Certification Level Unlocks in Warranty Coverage

The difference between certification tiers is not cosmetic. It directly determines which warranty the contractor can register on your behalf.

At the GAF level, the distinction is between System Plus (Certified Contractor, up to 50-year non-prorated shingle warranty, 10-year workmanship) and Golden Pledge (Master Elite only, up to 30-year non-dollar-limited workmanship warranty). For a roof in a coastal Florida environment where wind and UV exposure are extreme, the workmanship coverage period matters.

A No Dollar Limit (NDL) warranty — available through GAF's Golden Pledge program — means the manufacturer pays actual repair costs without a cap. A prorated warranty means coverage decreases in value each year after installation. On a 25-year-old roof that develops an installation defect in year 15, the payout under a prorated warranty may cover only a fraction of the repair.

Ask the contractor: which warranty tier can you register for my project, and what are the specific coverage terms? Request the warranty certificate language — not a summary, the actual certificate. Read the exclusions section. Know whether wind speed ratings apply and at what coverage level.

7. Re-Inspect Post-Storm: Storm Chasers Are Almost Never Certified

Northeast Florida gets hit. After Hurricane Irma, Matthew, and Ian, the contractor ecosystem shifted temporarily as out-of-state operators moved into the Jacksonville market. The pattern repeats after every significant storm event.

Storm chasers follow a business model: arrive in the days after a storm, sign insurance assignment-of-benefits (AOB) agreements or direct-pay contracts, collect insurance proceeds, and move on to the next event. They are not community contractors. They are not building long-term reputations in Duval County. And they are almost never manufacturer-certified.

The certification lookup takes five minutes. Run it before signing anything, particularly in the weeks after a named storm. A contractor who cannot be found in the GAF, CertainTeed, or Owens Corning locator is working without manufacturer backing — which means any warranty they hand you is their own, with no manufacturer standing behind it.
