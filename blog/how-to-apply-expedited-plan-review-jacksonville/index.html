How to Apply for Expedited Plan Review in Jacksonville

May 4, 2026 · 7 min read · JK Prestige Constructor Corp

Standard plan review in Duval County can run 4 to 8 weeks for residential projects and longer for complex commercial work. When a project timeline is fixed — a tenant's lease start date, a hospital department move, a commercial opening — that wait is not acceptable. Jacksonville offers an expedited review track, but the application process has specific eligibility rules, fee requirements, and common pitfalls that, if missed, send you back to the standard queue.

Step 1: Confirm Eligibility Before Applying

Not every project qualifies for expedited review. Jacksonville's Building Inspection Division currently offers expedited plan review for: commercial projects, tenant improvement (fit-out) projects, residential additions over a certain construction value (confirm the current threshold at coj.net, as thresholds are updated periodically), and certain renovation projects on existing commercial structures. Simple residential reroof permits and minor repairs generally do not qualify because they are already processed within days on the standard track. Call the Building Inspection Division at (904) 255-8500 or check the Division's current fee schedule page to confirm your project type qualifies before preparing your submission package.

Step 2: Prepare a Complete Drawing Set — No Exceptions

Expedited review is only faster if your drawing set is complete on first submission. Plan reviewers do not issue preliminary approvals or phase reviews under the expedited track — an incomplete set returns to the applicant with a deficiency letter, and the expedited clock stops. Your drawing set must include: a fully dimensioned site plan, architectural floor plans and elevations, structural details (engineered where required by code), mechanical, electrical, and plumbing plans for the scope, Florida Product Approval documentation for windows, doors, and roofing materials, and any required energy compliance calculations. Have a licensed architect or engineer review for completeness before submission.

Step 3: Submit the Expedited Review Request with the Fee

Log into the Duval County CityView portal and apply for your permit as usual. During the application, select the expedited review option when prompted for review type. You will pay both the standard plan review fee and the expedited fee simultaneously. Expedited fees for commercial projects in Jacksonville typically run $500 to $2,000 depending on construction valuation — the exact fee schedule is published on the Building Inspection Division's website and recalculated annually. For a commercial tenant fit-out valued at $300,000, expect an expedited fee in the $800 to $1,200 range on top of standard permit fees. Residential expedited fees are lower but still add $250 to $500 for qualifying addition projects.

Step 4: Target the 5–7 Business Day Review Window

Once your complete package is accepted and fees are paid, the expedited track targets a 5 to 7 business day plan review turnaround. That clock starts from the date your application is confirmed complete — not the date you submitted. If your submission has minor deficiencies, the reviewer will send a comment letter within the first 2 to 3 business days, which pauses the clock. Respond immediately and completely to comments; a second round of revisions typically drops the project from expedited status back to the standard queue.

Step 5: Identify What Triggers Delays Even on the Expedited Track

Certain issues will slow or stall even an expedited review. The most common: projects that require referral to another agency (fire marshal review, Environmental Protection Board review, St. Johns River Water Management District for sites with wetland proximity) cannot be expedited past those agencies — their review timelines are independent. Projects that change scope after submission are treated as new applications. Projects with code compliance issues that require interpretation by the Building Official rather than a line reviewer typically take longer regardless of fee paid. Know these triggers before you apply and resolve them upstream.

Step 6: Perform a Cost vs. Benefit Analysis Before Committing

Expedited plan review is the right choice when schedule delay costs more than the expedited fee. For a tenant who is paying rent on an unoccupied space, six weeks of holding costs on 5,000 square feet at $25 per square foot per year is roughly $14,400 — against an expedited fee of $1,000, the math is straightforward. For a homeowner adding a bedroom addition with no fixed deadline, the expedited fee may not be worth it. Calculate your holding cost or schedule penalty before deciding. Your contractor should be able to help you model this.

---

Pro Tips

  • Submit your permit application on a Monday or Tuesday. Applications submitted late in the week may not be assigned a reviewer until the following Monday, effectively losing 3 to 4 days of the expedited window.
  • If your project involves both building and fire sprinkler systems, the fire review runs on its own track. Expediting building plan review does not expedite fire review.
  • Keep a copy of every drawing set version submitted. If comments require revisions, the revised drawings must be clearly marked as revised with revision clouds and dates.
