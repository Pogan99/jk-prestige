What a Bathroom Addition Costs in Jacksonville in 2026

June 5, 2026 · 6 min read · JK Prestige Constructor Corp

Jacksonville homeowners adding a bathroom face the same problem: everyone gives a different number and nobody explains why. National average figures on home improvement sites are meaningless — they don't reflect Jacksonville labor rates, Florida permit requirements, or what a slab-foundation home actually costs when you have to cut concrete for drain lines.

These are honest 2026 cost ranges for bathroom additions in Jacksonville, explained by what drives each number.

Half Bath Addition (Powder Room): $18,000 to $32,000

A half bath is a toilet and sink, no shower or tub. It's the most common bathroom addition in Jacksonville because it adds significant resale value to homes that have only one full bathroom — and the scope is substantially simpler than adding a shower.

What's included in this range:

  • Framing (converting a closet, hall nook, or creating space from an adjacent room)
  • Plumbing rough-in: toilet drain, sink drain, cold and hot supply
  • Electrical: GFCI circuit, exhaust fan, vanity lighting
  • Drywall, primer, paint
  • Tile or LVP flooring
  • Standard toilet (elongated, comfort height)
  • Single vanity with undermount sink
  • Mirror and accessories
  • Permit fees

What drives it toward $32,000:

  • Slab cutting for drain line extension
  • Long drain run to reach the main stack
  • Structural framing modifications (not just a closet conversion)
  • Upgraded fixtures and finishes

What brings it toward $18,000:

  • Pier-and-beam foundation (no concrete cutting)
  • Closet conversion with existing drain proximity
  • Standard builder-grade fixtures and finishes

Full Bath Addition: $28,000 to $55,000

A full bath adds a shower or tub to the toilet and sink scope. This introduces waterproofing requirements that significantly affect both cost and the skill level required of the contractor.

What's included: All half bath scope, plus:

  • Shower pan, curb, and waterproofing membrane (or pre-sloped shower base)
  • Tile shower surround (standard 4x12 or subway tile)
  • Shower valve and trim
  • Tub if required
  • Exhaust fan sized for the space

What drives it toward $55,000:

  • Large custom tile shower (12x24 format or custom stone)
  • Frameless glass shower enclosure ($2,500 to $5,500 depending on size and configuration)
  • Soaking tub with separate shower
  • Premium fixtures (Kohler, Moen at the high end)
  • Slab cutting for extended drain run

What brings it toward $28,000:

  • Shower pan liner system (less expensive than topical waterproofing membrane)
  • Standard 4x4 or subway tile
  • Semi-frameless or curtain rod enclosure
  • Builder-grade fixtures
  • Pier-and-beam foundation, short drain run

Master Bath Addition: $45,000 to $90,000+

A master bath addition — meaning a full bath built as part of a primary bedroom suite or attached to an existing primary bedroom — is a different project than a standard full bath. The finish level expectations in Jacksonville's renovation market for master baths run significantly higher.

Standard master bath addition scope:

  • Double vanity (60" to 72" with undermount sinks)
  • Large format tile shower (12x24 or larger, possibly floor-to-ceiling)
  • Frameless glass shower enclosure
  • Soaking tub (separate from shower in most master bath configurations)
  • Private water closet (separate toilet compartment) — optional
  • Heated floor — optional
  • Exhaust fan on timer
  • Full electrical: vanity lighting, sconces, can lights

What drives it above $90,000:

  • Tile or natural stone throughout (heated floor + full shower in 12x24 stone)
  • Custom vanity cabinetry (shop-built, not stock)
  • Standalone soaking tub with floor-mounted tub filler
  • Expansion of the primary bedroom footprint to accommodate the larger bath (involves framing addition scope)
  • Private water closet with pocket door and separate exhaust

The Slab Factor: Jacksonville-Specific Cost Driver

The majority of Jacksonville homes built after 1960 are slab-on-grade foundations. Adding a bathroom in a location where drain lines don't already exist requires saw-cutting the concrete slab, excavating for the new drain line, running the pipe to the stack or building drain, and patching the concrete.

Slab cutting for bathroom drain addition: $3,000 to $8,000 depending on run length and access conditions.

Homes in Springfield, Riverside, Avondale, and older Murray Hill often have pier-and-beam foundations. Drain lines in these homes run under the floor in accessible crawl space — no concrete cutting. Slab cutting cost doesn't apply.

Ask your contractor directly: "Does my project require slab cutting, and is that included in your estimate?"

JK Prestige includes slab cutting in the fixed-price estimate when it's required. We assess this during the site visit — it's not a discovery charge added after the contract is signed.

What's Not in These Numbers

These ranges cover contractor-supplied labor and standard materials. What they don't include:

  • High-end fixtures you supply yourself (some homeowners purchase their own toilet, vanity, or plumbing fixtures)
  • Luxury tile (standard ceramic and porcelain are included; designer stone adds $15 to $40 per sqft)
  • Smart features (heated towel bars, smart mirrors, whole-room audio)
  • Any structural work beyond what the bathroom addition requires
  • Landscaping or exterior changes that result from a room addition
