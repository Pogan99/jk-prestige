What Does Roof Replacement Cost in Jacksonville FL? Price Ranges by Material and Roof Size

May 4, 2026 · 7 min read · JK Prestige Constructor Corp

The roof replacement cost question has a real answer — but it's a range, not a number. The material, the roof size, the pitch, the number of existing layers being torn off, the condition of the decking underneath, and the permit jurisdiction all affect the final price.

This post gives real installed cost ranges by material for both residential and commercial roofing in Jacksonville FL in 2025, explains what drives variation within each range, and covers what JK Prestige's free 24-point roof inspection includes.

---

Residential Roof Replacement Cost in Jacksonville FL

Residential roofing prices are quoted per square foot installed — meaning labor and materials combined, including tear-off of one existing layer. The ranges below reflect JK Prestige's installed pricing in Jacksonville and Duval County in 2025.

Asphalt Shingle — 3-Tab: $4 to $6 per Square Foot

Three-tab shingles are the entry-level asphalt product — flat-profile, single-thickness shingles with a 25-year product warranty (prorated). They meet Florida Building Code wind resistance requirements at the base level.

At $4 to $6/sqft installed, a 2,000 sqft roof (2,000 square feet of roof surface area, which is larger than the footprint on most homes due to pitch) runs $8,000 to $12,000. Three-tab is appropriate for a house that needs a functional roof within a tight budget. It is not the best long-term value in Florida's UV and storm environment.

Architectural Shingle: $6 to $9 per Square Foot

Architectural (dimensional) shingles have a laminated, textured profile that improves aesthetic appearance and provides better wind resistance than three-tab. Most manufacturers' architectural lines carry 30-year to lifetime limited product warranties.

At $6 to $9/sqft installed, a 2,000 sqft roof runs $12,000 to $18,000. This is the most common replacement choice for Jacksonville homeowners who want a quality roof without premium material cost.

GAF Timberline HDZ Premium: $8 to $11 per Square Foot

The GAF Timberline HDZ is an advanced architectural shingle with LayerLock technology — a factory-sealed tab that provides Class F wind resistance (rated for 130 mph winds, which aligns with Duval County's design wind speed). JK Prestige is a GAF Master Elite contractor, which means we can install Timberline HDZ with the System Plus or Golden Pledge manufacturer warranty — the highest warranty tier GAF offers.

At $8 to $11/sqft installed, a 2,000 sqft roof runs $16,000 to $22,000. The premium over standard architectural shingle is justified for homeowners who intend to own the property long-term and want manufacturer warranty coverage that transfers to a buyer.

The Golden Pledge warranty (25-year workmanship, lifetime product) is only available through GAF Master Elite contractors. JK Prestige holds that certification.

Standing Seam Metal Roof: $18 to $28 per Square Foot

Standing seam metal roofing is the most durable residential roofing system available for Florida's climate: Class 4 impact resistance, 130+ mph wind resistance, 40 to 70 year service life, and significantly better performance in high-humidity environments than asphalt products.

At $18 to $28/sqft installed, a 2,000 sqft roof runs $36,000 to $56,000. The upfront premium over architectural shingle is significant. The long-term economics are favorable: a standing seam roof that lasts 50 years requires no intermediate replacement that an asphalt roof would need at year 25.

Standing seam metal also carries insurance premium benefits in Florida — some carriers discount wind coverage for metal roofs rated to 150+ mph.

Slate: $20 to $35 per Square Foot

Natural slate is the premium residential roofing material: 75 to 150 year service life (with proper installation on a structurally adequate deck), unmatched aesthetics, and complete resistance to UV degradation and biological growth.

At $20 to $35/sqft installed, a 2,000 sqft slate roof runs $40,000 to $70,000. The cost reflects both the material price (premium quarried slate, often imported from Spain, Vermont, or Wales) and the labor — slate installation requires specialized skill that is not common in Jacksonville's market.

Slate is appropriate for custom homes where the architecture calls for it and the budget supports the upfront investment. The structural roof system must be capable of carrying the significantly higher weight of slate (8 to 14 lbs/sqft) compared to asphalt (2 to 3 lbs/sqft).

Commercial Flat Roof Replacement Cost in Jacksonville FL

Commercial flat roofs use membrane systems rather than shingles. Cost ranges are per square foot installed, including tear-off of one existing layer.

TPO (Thermoplastic Polyolefin): $7 to $12 per Square Foot

TPO is the dominant commercial flat roof membrane in Florida. White TPO reflects solar radiation, reducing cooling loads significantly in Florida's climate. TPO welds at seams using hot air rather than adhesives, producing a watertight seam that, when installed correctly, is extremely durable.

At $7 to $12/sqft installed on a 10,000 sqft commercial roof: $70,000 to $120,000. The range reflects insulation specification (single layer vs. tapered insulation for improved drainage), membrane thickness (45 mil vs. 60 mil vs. 80 mil), fastening method (mechanically attached vs. fully adhered), and manufacturer warranty tier.

JK Prestige installs TPO with Carlisle SynTec and Firestone UltraPly product lines and can certify the installation for manufacturer warranties up to 20 years.

EPDM (Ethylene Propylene Diene Monomer): $8 to $13 per Square Foot

EPDM is a black or white synthetic rubber membrane. It has an excellent track record for flexibility and seam integrity across temperature extremes. White EPDM provides similar reflectivity benefits to TPO. EPDM is typically fully adhered (bonded to the substrate with adhesive) rather than mechanically attached.

At $8 to $13/sqft, a 10,000 sqft EPDM re-roof runs $80,000 to $130,000. The premium over TPO reflects the fully adhered installation method, which is more labor-intensive but produces better wind uplift resistance.

Modified Bitumen: $6 to $10 per Square Foot

Modified bitumen (mod-bit) is a layered asphalt-based membrane typically applied in two or more plies. Torch-applied or cold-process adhesive installation. Modified bitumen has been a commercial roofing standard for decades — it's not the newest technology, but well-installed mod-bit on a properly prepared deck performs reliably.

At $6 to $10/sqft, a 10,000 sqft modified bitumen re-roof runs $60,000 to $100,000. Appropriate for low-slope commercial roofs where budget is the primary driver and the building owner is comfortable with the maintenance cadence that extended bitumen service requires.

What Affects the Final Price

Roof Complexity

A simple gable roof with no dormers, no skylights, and no chimneys is the least expensive to replace. Each hip, valley, dormer, and penetration adds labor time and material cost. Complex rooflines with multiple intersections can add 15 to 30% to the base per-sqft price.

Roof Pitch

Steeper roofs require more crew time, more safety equipment (OSHA fall protection requirements above 4:12 pitch for residential), and more material waste at cuts. Pitches above 8:12 typically carry a steep pitch surcharge of $0.50 to $2.00/sqft over the base price.

Tear-Off Layers

Florida Building Code limits the number of asphalt shingle layers on a residential roof to two. If there are already two layers present, both must be removed before a new roof can be installed. Removing two existing layers costs more than removing one: $1.50 to $3.00/sqft additional for the second tear-off layer, plus additional dumpster and disposal cost.

Decking Condition

Once existing roofing is removed, the roof decking (typically 5/8" OSB or plywood) is inspected. Rotted, delaminated, or water-damaged decking must be replaced. Decking replacement runs $80 to $120 per 4x8 sheet installed. On an older Jacksonville home, it's not unusual to replace 10 to 30% of the deck on a full re-roof.

Permit Fees

Duval County requires a building permit for residential roof replacements. Permit fees are based on the value of the work: typically $150 to $400 for a standard residential re-roof. Inspections are included in the permit process. JK Prestige handles permit application and inspection scheduling on every project.
