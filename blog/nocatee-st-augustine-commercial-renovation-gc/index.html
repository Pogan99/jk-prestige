Commercial Renovation Contractors in Nocatee and St. Augustine: What St. Johns County Requires

June 6, 2026 · 6 min read · JK Prestige Constructor Corp

Nocatee and St. Augustine are two of the fastest-growing commercial corridors in Northeast Florida right now. New retail pads, medical office buildings, and mixed-use projects are delivering monthly, and behind them comes a wave of tenant renovation and owner-occupied fit-out work. If you are building out commercial space in St. Johns County — whether it is a suite in a Nocatee Town Center pad or a renovation of a historic building on St. George Street — here is what the permit and construction process looks like.

St. Johns County vs. City of Jacksonville Permitting

This distinction matters. Nocatee and most of southern St. Johns County fall under St. Johns County Building Services, not the City of Jacksonville's Building Inspection Division. The processes are different:

  • St. Johns County Building Services handles most unincorporated St. Johns County areas, including Nocatee
  • City of St. Augustine has its own building department for properties within city limits
  • Ponte Vedra Beach and adjacent communities may fall under either St. Johns County or specific municipal jurisdictions

Contractors who routinely pull permits in Duval County but have never submitted to St. Johns County Building Services sometimes underestimate the review schedule differences. St. Johns County has its own review timelines, checklist requirements, and inspector scheduling procedures.

JK works in both jurisdictions. A fixed-price contract includes the permit fees, and a principal tracks each permit through review — not a coordinator checking a dashboard.

Commercial Renovation Permit Requirements: St. Johns County

For a commercial tenant renovation or owner-occupied fit-out in St. Johns County, the typical permit package includes:

  • Architectural drawings — floor plan, reflected ceiling plan, finish schedule, door schedule
  • Structural drawings — if non-load-bearing walls are being removed or new openings are cut
  • Mechanical drawings — HVAC modifications, duct layout, equipment schedules
  • Electrical drawings — panel schedule, lighting plan, receptacle layout
  • Plumbing drawings — if fixture count changes or new plumbing is added
  • Energy compliance documentation — Florida's commercial energy code requires HVAC and lighting calculations for new work

First-submission review in St. Johns County typically runs 15-25 business days for commercial interior renovations. Completeness matters — incomplete sets go to the back of the queue.

ADA Compliance in Commercial Renovation

This is the item that surprises most building owners who have not done commercial renovation before. Under the ADA and Florida Building Code, a commercial renovation that exceeds 50% of the building's replacement value triggers full ADA upgrades to the entire building, not just the renovated area. Even below that threshold, the renovation area itself must be brought into full ADA compliance, and an accessible path from the site entrance to the renovated space must be provided.

For older buildings in St. Augustine — where the building stock includes pre-ADA construction — this path-of-travel requirement sometimes requires restroom upgrades, parking modifications, or ramp additions that were not in the original renovation scope.

JK's pre-construction review includes an ADA path-of-travel assessment before the fixed-price contract is signed. If the restrooms need to be upgraded as a condition of your tenant renovation, that is in the scope and price — not a change order mid-project.

Nocatee Commercial Construction: What Is Different

Nocatee is a master-planned community with its own architectural review requirements in addition to St. Johns County building permits. Commercial construction in Nocatee typically requires:

  • Nocatee Amenity Authority approval — Architectural Review Committee (ARC) review of exterior modifications, signage, and visible construction elements
  • Landlord approval — Most Nocatee commercial buildings have landlord-mandated construction criteria specifying MEP systems, ceiling heights, utility connections, and construction hours
  • Access coordination — Nocatee's road network and access control infrastructure require advance coordination for material deliveries, dumpster placement, and crew staging

JK has worked in Nocatee commercial builds. The coordination overhead is real and is baked into the project schedule, not discovered after mobilization.

St. Augustine Historic District: A Different Compliance Layer

Commercial renovation within St. Augustine's Historic District triggers an additional review layer: the St. Augustine Historic Architectural Review Board (HARB). Any exterior modification — including signage, storefront changes, window replacement, or facade work — requires HARB approval before a building permit is issued.

Interior renovations not visible from the public right-of-way generally do not require HARB review, but confirm with the City of St. Augustine Building Department before assuming.

HARB review adds 30-60 days to the permit process. If your project touches the exterior of a contributing historic building, plan for it.

What a Typical Commercial Renovation Scope Looks Like

For a 1,500-3,500 sqft commercial tenant fit-out or owner-occupied renovation in the Nocatee/St. Augustine corridor:

  • Demolition of existing partitions and finishes
  • New metal stud framing, drywall, and finish carpentry — JK self-performs all three trades in-house
  • HVAC modifications — duct extension or replacement coordinated with mechanical sub
  • Electrical rough-in and finish — panel modifications, lighting, receptacles
  • Plumbing modifications — if breakroom, restroom, or wet area is included
  • Flooring, paint, millwork, doors, and hardware
  • Signage rough-in — conduit and blocking for tenant signage systems

JK self-performs framing, drywall, and finish carpentry. That is not brokered out to a sub whose schedule is theirs to control. JK crews show up on the JK schedule.

Timeline for a Commercial Renovation in St. Johns County

  • Pre-construction (drawings, ADA review, permit submittal): 3-6 weeks
  • Permit review (St. Johns County or City of St. Augustine): 3-6 weeks
  • Construction: 4-8 weeks depending on scope
  • Punch list and final inspection: 1-2 weeks
  • Certificate of Occupancy: 3-5 days after final inspection passes

Total elapsed time from project kick-off to CO: 3-4 months for a straightforward tenant fit-out, 4-6 months for projects requiring HARB review or significant MEP work.
