Warehouse Construction Cost Breakdown for Florida Developers in 2025

June 8, 2026 · 9 min read · JK Prestige Constructor Corp

The headline number — $65 to $90 per square foot for a warehouse in Florida — is useful for early feasibility. The line-by-line breakdown is what you need to make a financial decision, compare bids, or understand where value engineering is possible without compromising function.

JK Prestige delivers fixed-price warehouse construction across Northeast Florida. Here is a detailed cost breakdown for a 50,000-square-foot tilt-up distribution warehouse in Duval or Nassau County in 2025.

Base Project: 50,000 SF Tilt-Up Distribution Warehouse

Specs:

  • 50,000 SF total
  • 32-foot clear height
  • 10 dock-high doors, 2 grade-level doors
  • 4,000 SF office and break room (included in footprint)
  • 185-foot truck court depth
  • ESFR sprinkler system
  • LED high-bay lighting
  • Tilt-up concrete construction

---

Category 1: Site Work

Site work varies more than any other line item in warehouse construction, because it depends entirely on the specific site conditions.

Grading and earthwork: $180,000 to $420,000 Clearing, grubbing, cut and fill to achieve design grades, and final grading around the building. Sites that require significant fill (low-lying Northeast Florida properties) come in at the high end.

Paving (truck court, parking, drives): $280,000 to $380,000 Truck courts for a 50,000 SF warehouse: approximately 40,000 SF of 8-inch concrete pavement for heavy truck areas. Employee parking (100 to 150 spaces): asphalt, approximately 1.5 acres.

Stormwater management (retention pond, inlets, piping): $120,000 to $220,000 SJRWMD stormwater treatment requirements, retention pond sizing, inlet and pipe network. Sites with available stormwater credit from a basin management plan come in lower.

Utilities (water, sewer, electric service): $60,000 to $180,000 Highly variable. Established industrial corridor with available utilities: $60,000 to $90,000. Greenfield site requiring main extension: $100,000 to $180,000 or more. JEA transformer and meter set: $15,000 to $40,000 depending on service amperage.

Landscaping and irrigation: $25,000 to $45,000 Minimal, but required for Duval County site plan approval.

Site work subtotal: $665,000 to $1,245,000 Per SF of building: $13.30 to $24.90

---

Category 2: Building Shell

Tilt-up concrete panels (materials and labor): $850,000 to $1,100,000 Panels range from 7 to 9 inches thick for 32-foot height. Panel casting, tilt, and bracing. Panel cost varies with finish specification — exposed aggregate or reveal pattern adds cost.

Structural steel (roof framing, joists, purlins): $380,000 to $480,000 Open-web steel joists at 60-foot bay spacing with structural steel main frames. Heavier framing for high-piled storage loads.

Roof deck (metal deck): $180,000 to $230,000 22-gauge metal deck, standard B or F deck profile, factory prime painted.

Roofing (TPO membrane, insulation, flashings): $320,000 to $450,000 60-mil TPO over polyisocyanurate insulation at R-25 (Florida Energy Code minimum). Penetration flashings, curbs, and edge metal. Skylights optional.

Tilt-up panel connections, grout, caulk: $65,000 to $90,000 Welded connections at columns, horizontal joints, and corners. Backer rod and low-modulus sealant at panel joints.

Shell subtotal: $1,795,000 to $2,350,000 Per SF of building: $35.90 to $47.00

---

Category 3: Concrete Floor Slab

Slab-on-grade (7" with fibermesh, laser screed, FF/FL spec): $350,000 to $480,000 50,000 SF at $7.00 to $9.60 per SF. Includes subgrade prep, vapor barrier, doweled construction joints, control joints, fibermesh reinforcement, laser screed placement, and light broom finish. Hard-trowel finish for heavy traffic areas: add $0.50 to $1.00/SF.

Dock pits (if recessed dock levelers): $35,000 to $55,000 Hydraulic dock leveler pits require recessed concrete construction. 10 dock positions.

Slab subtotal: $385,000 to $535,000 Per SF: $7.70 to $10.70

---

Category 4: Building Systems

ESFR sprinkler system (installed, including pump if needed): $250,000 to $380,000 ESFR K-25 heads at 12-foot on-center, main drains, fire pump if water supply inadequate. Larger if JEA water supply requires a pump. Includes connection to JEA main and fire department connection.

LED high-bay lighting (400 fixtures, controls): $110,000 to $165,000 400-watt equivalent LED high-bays at 30-foot spacing in warehouse area. Daylight harvesting controls, occupancy sensors. Compliant with Florida Energy Code.

HVAC for office/break room: $45,000 to $75,000 4,000 SF office area with split system or packaged rooftop units. Warehouse area: unit heaters for winter, ventilation fans (evaporative coolers optional but not typically required in Jacksonville's mild winter climate).

Electrical (distribution, panels, outlets, yard lighting): $185,000 to $265,000 Main distribution panel at 2,000A-3,000A 480V three-phase, branch panels, warehouse outlets, exterior security and yard lighting.

Plumbing (restrooms, breakroom, hose bibs): $65,000 to $95,000 Two ADA-compliant restrooms, breakroom sink, utility sink, roof drain downspout connections.

Building systems subtotal: $655,000 to $980,000 Per SF: $13.10 to $19.60

---

Category 5: Dock Equipment

Hydraulic dock levelers (10 positions): $85,000 to $130,000 $8,500 to $13,000 per position for hydraulic leveler, dock bumpers, and dock seal installed.

Grade-level doors (2 positions): $15,000 to $25,000 12x14 or 14x14 sectional overhead doors with electric operators.

Dock equipment subtotal: $100,000 to $155,000 Per SF: $2.00 to $3.10

---

Category 6: Office Build-Out

4,000 SF office and break room (standard finish): $140,000 to $220,000 Metal stud framing, drywall, paint, LVP flooring, suspended ceiling, recessed lighting, HVAC, electrical, plumbing (2 restrooms, break room). Standard commercial finishes.

Higher specification (glass partitions, better finishes): $200,000 to $350,000.

---

Category 7: Soft Costs

Architectural and engineering design: $80,000 to $130,000 Civil, structural, architectural, MEP engineering. Tilt-up panel engineering is a specialty line item.

Permit fees (Duval County): $35,000 to $65,000 Building permit based on construction value. Impact fees vary by zone.

Geotechnical investigation and special inspections: $20,000 to $35,000 Soil borings, bearing capacity report, slab subgrade compaction testing, tilt-up panel special inspections.

Survey and boundary: $8,000 to $15,000

Contingency (5-8% of direct cost): $200,000 to $400,000

Soft costs subtotal: $343,000 to $645,000

Where Value Engineering Is Real and Where It Is Not

Real VE opportunities:

  • Site selection on a better-prepared site saves $200,000 to $400,000 in site work
  • Narrowing the bay spacing from 60 feet to 50 feet reduces steel cost
  • Delaying dock door count (rough in for 10, install 6 now) defers cost
  • Spec TPO at 60 mil instead of 80 mil where the application supports it

VE that looks good and costs more later:

  • Reducing slab thickness without racking load analysis
  • Eliminating secondary drainage (creates structural risk)
  • Reducing ESFR to standard wet-pipe without sprinkler engineer analysis
  • Specifying smaller dock levelers than the dock door height requires
