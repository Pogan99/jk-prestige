Custom Home Cost Per Square Foot in Jacksonville FL: What the Number Actually Includes

May 4, 2026 · 7 min read · JK Prestige Constructor Corp

"How much per square foot?" is the first question almost every custom home buyer asks. It's also one of the least useful questions you can ask, because per-square-foot cost is a summary of hundreds of decisions that haven't been made yet. Two 4,000-square-foot custom homes in Jacksonville can cost $1.4 million and $3.2 million respectively — built on different lots, with different foundations, different finishes, and different systems — and both are accurately described as "custom homes."

This post explains what the per-square-foot number does and doesn't include, what drives the Jacksonville custom home cost range, and what specific selections move the number most.

---

What Per-Square-Foot Cost Does Not Include

Before looking at ranges, understand what the headline per-square-foot number almost never includes.

Land: The lot is not in the construction cost per square foot. A lot in a gated community in Ponte Vedra Beach costs materially more than one in a developing subdivision in Orange Park. That difference doesn't show up in the per-sqft construction number.

Site preparation: Clearing and grubbing, grading, utility connections (water, sewer, electric, gas), and driveway installation are site costs — and in Florida, they vary dramatically. A lot in a coastal flood zone may require fill, compaction, and an elevated foundation that adds $50,000 to $150,000 to the project cost before the first frame board goes up. A lot in a developed neighborhood with utilities at the property line may have $20,000 in site prep. Neither of those is in the "construction cost per sqft."

Pool and outdoor living: A pool, spa, outdoor kitchen, screened lanai, and paved outdoor living area are common in Jacksonville custom homes and can add $100,000 to $350,000 to the project. They are almost never included in the per-sqft construction figure quoted at the beginning of a project.

Design fees: Architect or designer fees typically run 5% to 12% of construction cost. On a $1 million home, that's $50,000 to $120,000 in addition to construction.

Furniture and electronics: No construction contract includes furniture, home theater equipment, or anything the owner purchases and installs separately.

When you hear a per-sqft number, your first question should be: "Does that include the site, the pool, and the outdoor areas?" In most cases, the answer is no.

---

What Is Typically Included in Per-Square-Foot Cost

A construction-cost-only per-sqft figure typically includes:

  • Foundation system
  • Framing (structural and non-structural)
  • Exterior envelope (roofing, exterior walls, windows, doors)
  • MEP systems (Mechanical, Electrical, and Plumbing) rough-in and fixtures
  • Insulation
  • Drywall
  • Interior finishes (flooring, tile, millwork, countertops, paint)
  • Kitchen cabinets and appliances
  • Hardware, fixtures, and accessories
  • Interior doors and trim
  • Attached garage
  • General conditions, overhead, and GC fee

It does not include the land, the pool, the detached structures (guest house, workshop), landscaping, or furniture.

Real Cost Ranges by Custom Home Tier in Jacksonville FL

Standard Custom — $350 to $500 per Square Foot

This tier represents a well-built custom home with quality construction and mid-range to high-range finishes: 10-foot ceilings, hardwood or LVP throughout, engineered stone countertops, semi-custom cabinetry, standard lighting package, conventional HVAC, and standard structural systems.

At $350/sqft on a 3,000-sqft home, you're at $1,050,000 in construction costs — excluding land and site. At $500/sqft, the same home is $1,500,000. The difference is finish level: custom cabinetry vs. semi-custom, premium appliances vs. standard, higher-end tile and stone, upgraded plumbing fixtures.

Luxury Custom — $600 to $900 per Square Foot

Luxury custom construction adds:

  • Smart home systems: Integrated AV, lighting control, security, motorized shading, and sometimes geothermal or solar. These systems alone can add $30 to $80/sqft to the cost.
  • Higher structural complexity: Cathedral ceilings, complex rooflines, large spans that require engineered beams, and custom structural elements add material and labor cost.
  • Premium exterior materials: Full brick, natural stone, custom stucco, or a combination. Roofing upgrades to standing seam metal or slate.
  • Whole-house generator: $15,000 to $40,000 depending on size — nearly standard in this market tier in Florida.
  • Custom millwork throughout: Built-in shelving, coffered ceilings, custom trim profiles, wine cellar cabinetry, mudroom systems.
  • Premium MEP systems: Whole-house water treatment, multi-zone geothermal HVAC, in-floor radiant heat (in climate-controlled spaces), dedicated HVAC zones for wine storage.

A 5,000-sqft luxury custom home at $700/sqft is a $3.5 million construction project before land, pool, and design fees.

Ultra-Luxury — $1,000 per Square Foot and Above

At ultra-luxury, the per-sqft concept starts to break down because the scope is so custom. Projects in this tier involve multiple architects and specialty consultants, materials sourced internationally, custom fabrication throughout, and construction processes that don't conform to standard timelines.

JK Prestige's largest completed custom home to date was 11,200 sqft (the Hollowbrook Estate, NY, 2024). Projects at this scale involve detailed project programming — the specific requirements of the family — before any design begins, and fixed-price contracting only after design is complete and every scope item is defined.

The Florida-Specific Cost Factors

Foundation Type and Flood Zone

Florida homes in flood zones (and many Jacksonville areas have FEMA flood zone designations) require elevated foundations — either stem wall, piers, or slab elevated above the base flood elevation. The difference between a standard monolithic slab and an elevated foundation can be $30,000 to $80,000 on a 3,000-sqft home, depending on required elevation.

Check the FEMA flood map before pricing any Jacksonville lot. The required elevation above base flood level is a construction cost factor, not a design choice.

Hurricane-Rated Construction

Duval County is a 130 mph design wind speed zone. Impact-rated windows and doors (or a storm shutter system) are required. The premium for impact glass vs. standard windows runs $15,000 to $40,000 on a typical custom home. This is a Florida-specific cost that doesn't exist in most markets.

Concrete Block vs. Wood Frame

Florida custom homes are commonly built with concrete block (CBS — concrete block structure) at the exterior walls, which provides better wind resistance and pest resistance. Some luxury custom homes use structural concrete throughout for maximum durability. CBS construction adds 5 to 15% to the framing cost compared to wood frame but reduces insurance premiums and long-term maintenance.
