6 Reasons Jacksonville Is One of the Fastest-Growing Construction Markets in the Southeast

May 4, 2026 · 6 min read · JK Prestige Constructor Corp

Jacksonville does not get the national press coverage of Miami or Tampa, but the construction numbers tell a different story. Permit volumes in Duval County have grown every year since 2017, and 2024's commercial square footage under construction placed Jacksonville among the top five metros in the Southeast by active project count. That is not an accident — it is the result of six distinct forces all accelerating in the same market window. For contractors, developers, and owners, understanding what is driving the volume tells you where the labor pressure is and which project types are running on tight schedules.

JK Prestige Construction has been building in Jacksonville since 2017 — through every quarter of the current growth cycle and across 500+ completed projects. The drivers below are not projections. They are what is producing work in Duval, Nassau, St. Johns, and Clay counties right now.

1. Population Growth — 2nd Fastest-Growing Major Metro in Florida

Jacksonville added approximately 22,000 net new residents in 2023 alone and is tracking similarly for 2024. That makes it the second-fastest-growing major metro in Florida, behind only Orlando. Population growth at that rate translates directly to construction demand: new residential drives retail pad development, healthcare facilities, school construction, and the commercial infrastructure that serves a growing workforce. For commercial GCs, the more significant effect is the worker inflow — Jacksonville is attracting skilled trades from other Florida markets, which partially offsets the statewide labor shortage. But it also means more GCs competing for the same sites, permits, and material deliveries. Working with a GC that self-performs trades is, in this environment, a material schedule advantage.

2. JAXPORT Expansion — $300 Million in Capital Investment

JAXPORT's capital program at Blount Island and Dames Point has exceeded $300 million in recent years, with investments in crane systems, berth deepening, and terminal infrastructure. The port's capacity expansion is not just a logistics story — it is a construction catalyst. Every additional container throughput the port handles creates demand for warehouse and distribution facilities within 30 miles of the terminals. Nassau County's logistics corridor along SR 200 and US 1, and the I-95 industrial corridor through St. Johns County, are both absorbing that demand. JK has self-performed concrete and framing on multiple industrial projects in the JAXPORT-adjacent market. See our warehouse construction expertise for specifics.

3. Naval Station Mayport and NS Blount Island — Military Construction Activity

Naval Station Mayport is the third-largest naval surface fleet installation on the East Coast, and NS Blount Island is a Marine Corps prepositioning facility. Both generate steady federal construction demand: facility maintenance, barracks upgrades, operational building improvements, and periodic large-scale capital projects tied to fleet homeporting decisions. Military construction in Jacksonville does not track the same cycle as private commercial work — it is less sensitive to interest rates and more driven by Congressional appropriations. That makes it a stabilizing force in the local construction market during periods when private commercial slows. GCs operating in Jacksonville's military construction sector need specific bonding capacity and familiarity with federal procurement requirements.

4. Northeast Florida Healthcare Campus Expansion

Baptist Health South Florida, UF Health Jacksonville, and Mayo Clinic Florida have all announced or broken ground on significant Northeast Florida expansion projects in the last 24 months. Mayo Clinic's ongoing campus build-out in the San Pablo corridor alone represents hundreds of millions in construction over several phases. UF Health's downtown Jacksonville campus has active bed tower and outpatient renovation projects. Baptist's network of outpatient centers is expanding into St. Johns and Nassau counties. Healthcare construction is among the most technically demanding commercial work — ICRA (Infection Control Risk Assessment) protocols, NFPA 99 compliance, AHCA (Agency for Health Care Administration) oversight — and requires a GC with documented hospital experience. JK's hospital expertise covers the requirements in detail.

5. No State Income Tax Driving High-Net-Worth Relocation and Custom Home Demand

Florida's zero state income tax continues to drive relocation from high-tax states — primarily New York, New Jersey, Illinois, and California — with Jacksonville capturing a growing share of that inflow relative to South Florida. High-net-worth relocatees arriving in Duval and St. Johns counties bring custom home demand at price points that support full self-perform construction, finish carpentry at a higher specification, and longer construction schedules than production housing. The Ponte Vedra, Nocatee, and Julington Creek areas of St. Johns County have seen consistent demand for custom and semi-custom residential at the $800,000–$2.5 million range. JK's finish carpentry capability makes us a realistic option for custom work that requires the principal's involvement at every site visit — which is how we operate on every project.

6. Affordable Land Relative to Miami and Tampa Creating Developer Interest

Jacksonville land prices remain well below those in Miami, Tampa, and Orlando on a per-acre basis, even after five years of appreciation. For regional and national developers underwriting industrial, multifamily, or mixed-use projects, Jacksonville's land economics produce better returns on cost than most comparable Southeast metros. That developer interest — from Atlanta-based, Dallas-based, and nationally operating real estate funds — is producing a pipeline of projects that Jacksonville-based GCs are well-positioned to capture. Local knowledge matters in Jacksonville: understanding Duval County's development review process, which utility providers serve which corridors, and how the city's various Community Redevelopment Areas (CRAs) affect incentive availability is not something an out-of-market GC brings. FAQ

How long has JK Prestige Construction been operating in Jacksonville?

JK Prestige Construction was founded in 2017 and has operated continuously in Jacksonville and Northeast Florida through every year of the current growth cycle. We have completed 500+ projects across commercial, industrial, healthcare, and residential categories in Duval, Nassau, St. Johns, and Clay counties.

What types of construction projects does Jacksonville's JAXPORT expansion create?

JAXPORT's capacity expansion creates demand for industrial and logistics facilities — warehouse, distribution centers, truck terminals, and container yards — within 30 miles of the marine terminals. It also creates demand for infrastructure construction: road improvements, rail connections, and utility upgrades in the port-adjacent industrial corridors of Nassau and St. Johns counties.

Does military construction in Jacksonville require special licensing?

Yes. Federal military construction projects are procured through the U.S. Army Corps of Engineers or the Naval Facilities Engineering Systems Command (NAVFAC) and typically require bonding capacity, federal wage compliance under the Davis-Bacon Act, and — for classified facilities — security clearance for key personnel. GCs without prior federal contracting experience should approach military work through a teaming arrangement with a prime contractor that holds the required registrations.

Is healthcare construction growing in Jacksonville specifically, or across all of Florida?

Both, but Jacksonville is experiencing an outsized share of the growth relative to its market size. The presence of Mayo Clinic Florida, UF Health Jacksonville, and Baptist Health's regional expansion has created a concentration of healthcare construction activity in Northeast Florida that rivals South Florida on a per-capita basis. That concentration is drawing national healthcare GCs into the Jacksonville market, which increases competition for healthcare-trained crews and ICRA-certified supervisors.
