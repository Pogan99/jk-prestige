Kitchen Gut Renovation Cost in Jacksonville: What You'll Actually Pay in 2026

June 5, 2026 · 7 min read · JK Prestige Constructor Corp

Kitchen gut renovations are the most misquoted project type in residential construction. National averages are useless. The number you see on home improvement websites — "$25,000 to $50,000" — doesn't reflect Jacksonville labor rates, current material costs, or what "gut" actually means.

This is what a kitchen gut renovation costs in Jacksonville in 2026, broken down by scope.

What "Gut Renovation" Actually Means

A gut renovation means stripping the kitchen to the studs. Everything goes out: cabinets, countertops, flooring, drywall, and often the ceiling. Plumbing and electrical rough-in may be partially or fully replaced depending on the age of the home and the new layout.

This is different from:

  • A cabinet refacing job
  • A cosmetic update (new counters, painted cabinets)
  • A "refresh" (new appliances, new hardware, new paint)

If you're keeping any of the following — original cabinet boxes, original plumbing rough-in, original electrical — it's not a gut.

Jacksonville Kitchen Gut Renovation Cost Ranges

Entry-level gut renovation (keep layout, stock cabinets, standard appliances): $38,000 to $52,000

This covers demo, new drywall, stock or semi-custom cabinetry, quartz or granite countertops, standard plumbing fixtures, standard stainless appliances, and LVP or tile flooring. No layout change. No structural modifications.

Mid-range gut renovation (minor layout change, semi-custom cabinets, upgraded appliances): $55,000 to $80,000

Adds: partial wall removal or relocation, semi-custom cabinetry, island or peninsula if not previously existing, tile backsplash, upgraded range and refrigerator (sub-zero class or similar), and potentially a panel circuit addition for induction or high-BTU gas.

High-end gut renovation (new layout, custom cabinets, premium appliances): $85,000 to $130,000+

Custom cabinetry (built by a cabinet shop to your exact dimensions and specifications), Thermador or Wolf range, Sub-Zero refrigeration, full panel upgrade, structural modifications if walls move, possibly a window replacement to change the kitchen's natural light. If the kitchen opens to a living space with a structural header removal, add $8,000 to $20,000 for that scope.

What Drives Kitchen Renovation Cost in Jacksonville

1. Layout changes Moving the sink means rerouting supply and drain lines. Moving a gas range means extending gas lines. Moving the refrigerator means running new electrical. Every layout change adds trade scope. A layout redesign that seems simple on a drawing often costs $8,000 to $20,000 more in rough trade work than keeping appliances in place.

2. Structural modifications Opening a wall between the kitchen and dining room requires a structural beam. That beam — engineered, supplied, and installed with appropriate point loads transferred down to the foundation — runs $6,000 to $18,000 depending on span and load path. Permit required.

3. Cabinetry Cabinet cost is the single biggest variable in kitchen renovation. Stock cabinets from a big-box store run $150 to $400 per linear foot installed. Semi-custom runs $450 to $800. Custom shop cabinetry runs $900 to $1,800+ per linear foot. In a 15-foot kitchen with an island, the difference between stock and custom is $18,000 to $35,000.

4. Countertop material Standard granite or quartz: $70 to $120 per sqft installed. Quartzite or high-end marble: $150 to $300 per sqft installed. Waterfall island edges: add $800 to $2,000 per waterfall side.

5. Appliances Not always in the GC contract — some owners supply their own. Mid-range package (30" range, standard refrigerator, dishwasher, microwave): $5,000 to $10,000. High-end package (range, refrigerator, dishwasher, separate oven): $18,000 to $45,000.

Permits Required for a Kitchen Gut in Jacksonville

Kitchen gut renovations in Jacksonville require permits when the scope includes:

  • Electrical work (circuit additions, panel work)
  • Plumbing (any fixture relocation, drain or supply modifications)
  • Structural work (wall removal, beam installation)

Cosmetic work — new cabinets in same locations, new counters, new flooring — generally does not require a permit if no systems or structure are touched. However: if you're doing a true gut, you're almost certainly doing electrical and plumbing work.

Permit fees in Duval County for a kitchen renovation run approximately $400 to $1,200 depending on scope. JK Prestige includes permit costs in the fixed-price contract.
