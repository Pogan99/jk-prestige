What the Jacksonville FL Real Estate Boom Means for Custom Home Construction Lead Times

May 4, 2026 · 7 min read · JK Prestige Constructor Corp

---

Every part of the custom home supply chain in Jacksonville has been under sustained stress since 2021, and in 2025, that stress has not fully resolved. Duval County's residential plan review queue is longer than it was five years ago. The GCs worth hiring have booking windows that extend 6-12 months from first conversation to contract. Subcontractors for electrical and plumbing work are running 6-8 weeks out for new project starts. Structural windows take 12-16 weeks from order to delivery.

Understanding these lead times is not an academic exercise. Owners who plan a custom home build without accounting for them will break ground 12-18 months after they expected to — or they will break ground on schedule by compressing phases in ways that produce a worse outcome: GC selection under time pressure, materials ordered late, permits chasing construction rather than preceding it.

Here is the actual lead time picture for custom home construction in Jacksonville in 2025, and what JK Prestige's pre-construction phase locks in before ground breaks.

---

The Six Lead Times That Determine Your Custom Home Schedule

1. GC Availability: 6-12 Month Booking Windows

The GCs in Jacksonville who deliver custom homes at a quality level — self-perform trades, principal accountability, VDC (Virtual Design & Construction) on complex projects, fixed-price contracts — are not sitting idle waiting for the next project. They are booked.

The booking window for JK Prestige and comparable quality GCs in the Jacksonville market runs 6-12 months from initial conversation to contract signing. This does not mean 6-12 months before work starts — it means 6-12 months before you are in the queue for a start date.

What this means practically: if you want to break ground in March 2026, you need a GC conversation by June 2025 at the latest — and ideally earlier. Owners who call a GC the month before they want to start construction choose between accepting a lower-quality GC or delaying their groundbreaking.

JK Prestige's pre-construction phase begins at engagement, not at groundbreaking. The time between contract signing and groundbreaking is not dead time — it is when site validation, long-lead procurement, and permit submittal happen.

2. Residential Permit Review: 4-6 Weeks in Duval County

Duval County's Building Inspection Division reviews residential new construction permit applications in approximately 4-6 weeks for complete, code-compliant submissions. In 2019, that review period was 2-3 weeks. The increased timeline reflects the volume of residential construction applications the county is processing.

The 4-6 week window assumes a complete, first-submission-quality application. Re-submittals reset the clock. A permit application that comes back with a correction list and requires one re-submittal adds 4-6 weeks. Two re-submittals adds 8-12 weeks. JK Prestige submits complete drawings on first submission — our review process before submittal catches the issues that trigger correction lists.

Projects that require concurrent Fire Marshal review (sprinkler systems in larger homes), environmental permits (wetland setbacks, stormwater management), or water management district review add further time outside Duval County's standard residential queue.

3. Subcontractor Availability: 6-8 Weeks for New Project Starts

The electrical and plumbing subcontractors who perform quality work in Jacksonville are running 6-8 weeks out for new project starts. A GC who signs a contract and then begins subcontractor outreach at permit issuance is already 6-8 weeks behind on their schedule.

JK Prestige maintains ongoing trade partner relationships in Jacksonville. Our self-perform trades — framing, concrete, drywall, finish carpentry, and roofing — are not subject to subcontractor availability. Our own crews mobilize when the schedule calls for them. The MEP subs we work with regularly know our schedule standards and prioritize our projects accordingly.

The difference between a GC with established sub relationships and a GC who bids trades to whoever responds is 4-8 weeks of schedule compression on a 12-month custom home build.

4. Structural Windows: 12-16 Weeks

This is the lead time that surprises most homeowners. Windows — particularly custom or semi-custom residential windows in the profile sizes typical of custom home construction — have lead times of 12-16 weeks from order to delivery.

Anderson, Pella, Marvin, and equivalent manufacturers operating at the quality level of custom home construction are not keeping shelf inventory of custom-profile windows. They manufacture to order. A window order placed at permit issuance, when the drawings are finalized, delivers to the jobsite 12-16 weeks later — which may land in the middle of drywall if the schedule was not planned around it.

JK Prestige identifies windows as a long-lead item in pre-construction and places the order before permit issuance, using the drawings that will be submitted for permit. Window specs are locked at pre-construction, not after permit approval.

5. Structural Steel: 14-20 Weeks

Custom homes with structural steel elements — clear-span roof structures, large beam headers in open floor plans, steel moment frames for large window wall openings — carry steel lead times of 14-20 weeks in 2025.

Structural steel is fabricated to project-specific specifications. There is no shelf inventory of custom beam sizes. A steel fabricator needs the structural engineer's stamped drawings, fabricates to spec, and delivers to the schedule that their shop capacity allows. That process takes 14-20 weeks.

A custom home design with a structural steel element that is not identified and ordered in pre-construction will delay the installation of the roof structure by 14-20 weeks from when the order is placed — which, if placed at permit issuance, means the structure is under the weather longer than it needs to be and the schedule compresses every subsequent phase.

6. Long-Lead Finish Items: 8-16 Weeks

Custom cabinetry, specialty tile, and engineered hardwood flooring from quality manufacturers operate on similar lead times to windows: 8-16 weeks from order to delivery. The risk is less schedule-critical than windows and steel, but late orders on finish materials push the completion date of a custom home that is otherwise ready to punch list.

What This Means: Start Planning Now for a 2026 Ground Break

Owners who want to break ground in Q1 2026 need to be in active pre-construction now. Here is what the realistic timeline looks like:

  • Now through June 2025: GC engagement, site validation, pre-construction scope development
  • June-August 2025: Design development finalization, long-lead material identification, window and steel orders placed
  • August-October 2025: Permit documents finalized, permit application submitted
  • October-December 2025: Permit review and approval (4-6 weeks typical), window and steel delivery
  • Q1 2026: Ground break, site preparation, foundation

Compressing this sequence produces one of two outcomes: a later ground break than planned, or a compromised pre-construction process that creates schedule and cost problems during construction.
