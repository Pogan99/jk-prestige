Commercial Building Permit Process in Jacksonville FL: Timeline, Fees, and Inspection Stages

May 4, 2026 · 7 min read · JK Prestige Constructor Corp

---

Commercial construction in Jacksonville cannot begin until a building permit is issued, and that permit doesn't move on autopilot. It requires coordinated submittals through multiple city departments, multiple rounds of plan review comments, and precise inspection sequencing once work begins. Developers and building owners who don't understand the process in detail end up funding delays they could have avoided.

This post covers the full commercial permit process in Jacksonville: what's required at application, which departments review your drawings and why, how long each stage takes, what permit fees cost, and what each inspection stage covers.

---

The Jacksonville Commercial Permit Process: Overview

Commercial building permits in Jacksonville are administered by the City of Jacksonville Building Inspection Division through the CityView online portal. Unlike residential permits — which may qualify for streamlined review — commercial projects always require multi-department plan review.

The process has six stages:

  1. Pre-application meeting (optional but recommended)
  2. Application submittal through CityView
  3. Multi-department plan review (first round)
  4. Comments issued and revisions resubmitted
  5. Permit issued and fees paid
  6. Inspections through certificate of occupancy (CO)

A straightforward commercial project — a tenant improvement in an existing building, a single-story commercial shell — can move through stages 1-5 in 6-8 weeks with complete drawings and no significant comments. Complex projects — new construction over three stories, healthcare facilities, projects requiring traffic impact review — routinely take 12-18 weeks or more for permit issuance.

The schedule you build for commercial construction must include this timeline. A developer who plans to break ground 30 days after a permit application is submitted is not accounting for how Jacksonville's review process actually works.

---

Stage 1: Pre-Application Meeting

The City of Jacksonville Building Inspection Division offers pre-application meetings for complex commercial projects. This is an optional step, but it's one of the highest-value investments a commercial developer can make before submitting.

In a pre-application meeting, the design team presents the project concept, proposed code approach for complex issues (occupancy classification, construction type, means of egress, sprinkler system design basis), and any questions about local amendments or zoning compliance. The Building Inspection Division provides informal guidance on how reviewers will approach the project.

Projects that use the pre-application meeting receive fewer first-round comments. Comments are the primary source of permit delay — each round of comments resets a 3-6 week review clock. One well-prepared pre-application meeting can eliminate two rounds of comments, saving 6-12 weeks on the permit timeline.

JK Prestige recommends pre-application meetings for all new commercial ground-up projects and any project with complex code interpretations (healthcare, mixed-use, assembly occupancy).

---

Stage 2: Application Submittal Through CityView

Commercial permit applications are submitted electronically through the CityView portal at coj.net. The application package includes:

Required at submittal:

  • Completed building permit application
  • Construction documents signed and sealed by the architect and engineer(s) of record
  • Separate sealed drawings for: architectural, structural, civil (site), MEP (Mechanical, Electrical, and Plumbing) — each stamped by the appropriate licensed professional
  • Soils/geotechnical report (for new construction projects)
  • Contractor license information for GC and all licensed trade contractors
  • Energy code compliance documentation (Florida Energy Code compliance form)
  • Product approval numbers for any Florida Building Code Approval-required products (windows, roofing systems, etc.)

For projects with special requirements:

  • Fire protection system drawings (sprinkler, alarm) submitted to Jacksonville Fire Marshal separately
  • Stormwater management permit from the St. Johns River Water Management District (SJRWMD) if applicable
  • Traffic impact study if project exceeds defined vehicle trip thresholds (coordinated with JTA and city traffic engineering)

Incomplete submittals are returned without entering the review queue. A rejected submittal costs the time to correct and resubmit — plus whatever position the application lost in the queue. The GC's permit coordinator should verify completeness against the Building Inspection Division's current checklist before submitting.

---

Stage 3: Multi-Department Plan Review

Once an application enters the review queue, multiple departments review the drawings simultaneously. Each department has its own review scope and timeline:

Building Division: Structural compliance, construction type, occupancy classification, means of egress, accessibility, energy code. This is typically the longest review for complex projects.

Fire Marshal (Jacksonville Fire and Rescue): Egress widths, fire-resistance ratings, sprinkler system compliance, fire alarm system coordination, hazardous materials storage if applicable. Fire review runs on a separate track from building review — both must be complete before the permit issues.

Zoning Division: Use compliance, setbacks, lot coverage, height, parking supply versus demand, signage, landscaping (if required by the zoning ordinance).

Environmental/Engineering Division: Stormwater, grading, erosion control, environmental permits for sites near wetlands or water bodies.

Traffic Engineering: If a traffic impact study was required, traffic review may run concurrently with the above.

Review timeline by project type:

  • Tenant improvement (existing commercial building): 3-5 weeks first round
  • New commercial single-story: 5-8 weeks first round
  • New commercial multi-story: 6-10 weeks first round
  • Healthcare / medical facility: 8-14 weeks first round (AHCA review separate, adds time)
  • Projects requiring SJRWMD environmental permit: add 6-12 weeks for that permit track

---

Stage 4: Comments Issued and Revisions Submitted

After first-round review, each department issues comments. The design team must respond to every comment — some require drawing revisions, some require additional documentation, some require design changes.

Response and resubmittal timeline: The design team typically takes 2-4 weeks to prepare revisions for a routine comment set. Complex structural or code compliance comments can take longer.

Second-round review: After resubmittal, comments are reviewed again. Second-round review is typically shorter than first-round: 2-4 weeks for most projects. If the revisions adequately address all comments, the permit is approved. If new issues are raised or revisions are incomplete, a third round begins.

Projects with well-prepared initial submittals and straightforward code approaches typically resolve in 2 rounds. Projects with complex code issues or design changes triggered by first-round comments can go 3-4 rounds, adding months to the permit timeline.

---

Stage 5: Permit Issued and Fees Paid

When all departments approve the drawings, the permit is issued through CityView. Permit fees for commercial construction in Jacksonville are assessed against the declared construction value:

Permit fees: Approximately $10-$25 per $1,000 of construction value, depending on project type and scope. A $2 million commercial project can expect permit fees of $20,000-$50,000.

Additional fees:

  • Plan review surcharge (separate from permit fee)
  • Fire review fee (paid to Jacksonville Fire Marshal separately)
  • Zoning review fee
  • Stormwater review fee if applicable

Budget permit fees at 1.5-3% of construction cost for commercial projects in Jacksonville. Fees are paid before the permit is issued; the GC typically advances the fees and invoices the owner as a project cost.

---

Stage 6: Inspection Stages for Commercial Construction

Once the permit is issued and work begins, inspections are called at each defined construction stage. The GC is responsible for scheduling inspections through CityView before covering work.

Commercial inspection sequence:

  1. Site inspection: Erosion and sediment controls in place before earth disturbance. Setbacks verified against site plan.
  1. Foundation inspection: Formwork set, rebar in place, before concrete pour. Anchor bolt layout for structural connections verified.
  1. Rough framing inspection: Structural framing complete, before any sheathing or enclosure. Hurricane strapping, header sizing, and shear panel placement verified.
  1. Rough plumbing inspection: Supply and drain rough-in complete, pressure tested, before walls are closed. Separate inspection from rough electrical and HVAC.
  1. Rough electrical inspection: Panel rough-in, conduit runs, device boxes, before walls are closed. Load schedule and panel schedule verified against approved drawings.
  1. Rough HVAC inspection: Duct rough-in, equipment mounting, before walls are closed. Duct testing may be required depending on project type.
  1. Insulation inspection: Insulation installed, before drywall application.
  1. Pre-drywall inspection: Some inspectors require a pre-drywall walkthrough for commercial projects to verify all rough-in work is complete and documented before enclosure.
  1. Final plumbing, electrical, and HVAC inspections: Each trade's finished work inspected separately.
  1. Fire alarm and sprinkler system tests: Witnessed functional tests of all fire protection systems. Separate inspection by Jacksonville Fire Marshal.
  1. Final building inspection: All work complete. Fire, building, and accessibility all must pass.
  1. Certificate of occupancy issued: After all inspections pass. See JK Prestige's post on Duval County CO requirements for commercial construction for the full CO checklist.

Expedited Review: Cost and Time Savings

Jacksonville Building Inspection Division offers an expedited plan review program for commercial projects. Expedited review moves the application to the front of the queue — typically reducing review time by 30-50%.

Expedited review cost: Varies by project size and review departments involved. Commercial projects typically pay an expedited fee of $3,000-$10,000 on top of standard permit fees.

When expedited review is worth it: When general conditions costs per week exceed the expedited review fee. On a commercial project with $10,000/week in general conditions, a 3-week timeline savings justifies a $30,000 expedited fee. Most commercial projects where the schedule is important should use expedited review.
